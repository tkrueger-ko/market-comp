*Internal sales enablement. Partisan by design — built to win the deal — but every Fierce weakness cited here is sourced and real. Do not embellish past what's in the "Proof points & receipts" section; an exaggerated claim that gets caught loses more trust than the deal is worth.*

## 30-second positioning

Fierce is the rival you should take **most seriously on paper and least seriously on delivery.** Once you correct the published spec sheets for real errors, Fierce has the **best head-to-head spec record of any competitor** — it's genuinely light, genuinely carbon, and the accuracy guarantee is real. But flip to what owners actually report after the sale, and Fierce **ranks dead last of five brands on delivery (5.05/10 composite)** — "roll the dice... you get a shooter or a boat anchor." It's the one brand in the field where winning on the spec sheet and winning in the safe/at the range are two different questions. **The sale isn't "whose spec sheet is better" — it's "who will actually be holding the phone when this doesn't group out of the box, and what does it cost you to find out."** Horizon's job in this conversation is to move the prospect from comparing PDFs to comparing what happens after the credit card is charged.

## Where Fierce genuinely wins / looks good on paper (what reps must neutralize honestly)

Don't argue these — they're true, sourced, and arguing them is a losing, fact-checkable move that costs credibility.

- **Best corrected head-to-head spec record in the field.** Once the report's verified Fierce spec errors are fixed (CT Rival XP was misfiled as a 7 lb steel/0.75 MOA rifle when it's actually a **6 lb titanium-action, full carbon-barrel, 0.5 MOA** rifle; chamberings were undercounted ~half across all five in-scope models), Fierce tops more matchups than any other competitor, including two Horizon currently appears to win (Vandal Prime 2 narrows to a virtual tie/flips to Fierce; Vandal Carbon 2 and Venatic DM flip to Fierce). *(fierce.md, Net effect on rankings)*
- **Real weight advantage.** Fierce's carbon rifles run **5.3–6.5 lb**; Horizon's hunters run **7–7.5 lb**. On a mountain-rifle weight comparison, Fierce wins outright today — Horizon doesn't yet field a sub-6 lb option. *(CONSOLIDATED_ASSESSMENT.md §8, Tier 1 rec #3)*
- **Real price advantage.** Fierce models typically run **$1,000–$3,000 below** the comparable Horizon model (e.g., Carbon Rogue $2,150–2,199 vs. Venatic Carbon 2 $3,999, a 82%+ gap). Horizon is the highest- or tied-highest-MSRP rifle in 13 of 14 corrected matchups. *(fierce.md; CONSOLIDATED_ASSESSMENT.md §2.2)*
- **The accuracy guarantee is real and frequently met — not vaporware.** Independent owners and press have shot Fierce rifles to 0.22–0.64 MOA, beating spec with factory match ammo (Carbon Rogue "exceeded its 0.750-inch guarantee," 0.21" best; CT Rival 0.641" best-10 avg). *(sentiment_fierce.md, Accuracy-in-practice)*
- **Carbon-barrel feature set is genuinely loved when the rifle is right.** "Insanely accurate... instant favorite" (MTN Reaper); Outdoor Life's CT Rival "ejected every case positively" over ~400 rounds. This is not a fake selling point — owners who get a good copy are enthusiastic. *(sentiment_fierce.md, Top praised themes)*
- **The four newest SKUs (CT Rival XP, Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0) have almost no public owner-complaint data yet.** Don't claim these specific current-production models are "lemons" — the negative QC pattern is overwhelmingly documented on *older* models (Carbon Rogue 7PRC, legacy Mountain Reaper, Carbon Rival), not these four. Treat the newest SKUs as unproven, not condemned. *(sentiment_fierce.md, Summary & Coverage gap)*
- **A genuine quality-improvement effort exists.** Fierce ran a NIST-MEP Six Sigma/Kanban quality program in 2021 after its Canadian manufacturing partnership ended. It's real, but contested — near-identical complaint threads (incl. "Yet another Fierce Firearms Issue... 2024") continued well after. Acknowledge the effort; don't claim it failed outright, and don't claim it fixed the problem either. *(sentiment_fierce.md, Summary)*

## Horizon's winning wedges vs Fierce (top 4)

### 1. Promise vs. delivery — the single clearest gap in the entire competitive field
Fierce **tops the corrected spec scoring** but its researched owner-delivery composite is **5.05/10 — dead last of five brands audited**, against Horizon's **7.75/10 — first of five.** The brand that wins on paper is the one the market trusts least to honor the paper. This is not cherry-picked: it is corroborated across ~45–50 independent sources (Rokslide, Long Range Hunting, Long Range Only, Sniper's Hide, gun-press tests) over multiple years. *Proof: sentiment_fierce.md Composite table; CONSOLIDATED_ASSESSMENT.md §7.*

### 2. The $200 guarantee fee — the guarantee isn't free to invoke
Fierce's own accuracy-warranty process charges the customer **$200 + shipping** if Fierce can replicate the guaranteed group size in its own testing. Owners describe this as discouraging legitimate claims — "the guarantee isn't truly free." Pair this with documented multi-week-to-multi-month customer-service silence (one owner: "spoke with 5-6 employees and not one came through," 8 months; another: "never received an email reply," ~2 months). **Ask the prospect: what's the real cost of a guarantee you have to pay to invoke, and then wait months on?** *Proof: fiercearms.com/accuracy-warranty-form; sentiment_fierce.md Warranty & CS sub-score and Top complaint themes.*

### 3. Documented unit-to-unit QC variance — "shooter or boat anchor"
The single strongest, most recurring signal across independent forums is QC inconsistency: a Sniper's Hide thread sums it up as "roll the dice... you get a shooter or a boat anchor." A third-party gunsmith reportedly saw "no less than 5 Fierce rifles" with the same barrel/accuracy failure in one year, several requiring a warranty re-barrel — i.e., **the same accuracy guarantee that's supposed to be the headline can itself fail and need a hardware fix to resolve, not just a phone call.** One owner even reported Fierce's *own* factory test target measured 0.64 MOA on a rifle sold under a 0.5 MOA guarantee. *Proof: snipershide.com/shooting/threads/fierce-rifles-quality-and-accuracy.7214562; sentiment_fierce.md Build quality sub-score and Top complaint themes.*

### 4. Horizon's delivery lead is real, even though it's the underdog brand
Independent, non-Fierce-affiliated data (Backfire.tv's blind 20-shot accuracy database, Rifle Magazine, Petersen's Hunting) consistently measured Horizon rifles meeting or beating the 0.5 MOA promise, and Horizon's own composite of 7.75 leads the field on every dimension Fierce is weakest on — Warranty/CS (7.5 vs. Fierce's 4.0) and Build & reliability (7.5 vs. 5.0). **Caveat reps must carry into this pitch (see Landmines):** Horizon's data set is thin (~17 sources, no Reddit/BBB footprint) — sell it as "the limited but unanimous evidence says we deliver," not as "thousands of proven owners." *Proof: sentiment_horizon.md Composite table and Confidence section.*

## Discovery questions that surface Fierce's delivery risk

Ask these early — they let the prospect discover the gap themselves rather than hearing it as a Horizon talking point:

1. "If the rifle doesn't group to the advertised MOA out of the box, what's the actual process — and is there any cost to you to find out whether it qualifies?"
2. "What's the typical turnaround time you've heard about for a warranty claim or re-barrel on that brand?"
3. "Have you seen independent owner data — not the manufacturer's site — on the specific model and chambering you're considering, or mostly marketing copy and a few cherry-picked reviews?"
4. "Does the accuracy guarantee hold with the ammo you actually plan to hunt/compete with, or only with a specific factory match load?"
5. "If you get a rifle that needs a warranty fix, how many rifles would that take you out of the field for, and during what season?"
6. "Who do you actually talk to when something goes wrong — and how fast have other owners said that company responds?"

## Landmines — claims NOT to make

- **Don't claim Fierce's spec sheet or build quality is bad.** It isn't — corrected, Fierce has the strongest head-to-head spec record of any competitor, and the accuracy guarantee is real and frequently met. Arguing the spec sheet is a losing, easily-fact-checked move.
- **Don't claim the accuracy guarantee is fake or "never met."** It's regularly beaten by genuine owners and press (0.22–0.64 MOA results exist). The honest claim is *inconsistent*, not *false*.
- **Don't apply the QC-variance reputation to the four newest SKUs (CT Rival XP, Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0) as if they're individually proven bad.** The documented failures concentrate on older/different models (Carbon Rogue 7PRC, legacy Mountain Reaper, Carbon Rival). Frame it as a company-pattern/process risk, not a confirmed defect in the exact rifle on the table.
- **Don't claim Fierce has a bad BBB rating or a public complaint log.** Verified: Fierce's BBB profile is **A+ (not accredited)**; no complaint detail was found there. Don't invent or imply a bad BBB score — it's checkable and false.
- **Don't cite "Reddit says" about Fierce.** Researchers found essentially nothing on Reddit for this brand — it's not a real data source here.
- **Don't claim Horizon wins every Fierce matchup on spec.** It doesn't, even after correcting errors in Horizon's favor. Vandal Prime 2 is a near-tie that flips to Fierce under a fair accuracy-scoring rule; Vandal Carbon 2 and Vandal DM are Fierce wins, not close ones. If a prospect is comparing those specific models side by side, don't pretend Horizon has the edge on paper — pivot immediately to delivery.
- **Don't oversell Horizon's own delivery data as large-scale proof.** It rests on ~17 sources, no Reddit/BBB/Trustpilot footprint, and n=1–2 lab-tested rifles per model. Say "the evidence that exists is unanimous and independently sourced," not "thousands of happy owners" or "proven at scale."
- **Horizon's no-fee accuracy policy is now CONFIRMED — use it.** Horizon does **not** charge a fee for accuracy testing and backs every rifle with a no-questions commitment to make it right. This is a lead claim against Fierce's $200 fee. (Do still confirm exact turnaround/RMA logistics with service before quoting a specific number of days.)
- **Don't imply Horizon ships a per-rifle test target — we don't.** Our delivery proof is *independent third-party* testing (Backfire.tv, Rifle Magazine) plus the no-fee make-it-right commitment — not a target in the box. Attack the generalizability of a competitor's single test target; don't pretend we ship our own.
- **Weight: concede today, but sub-6 lb options are launching now.** Horizon's current hunters run 7–7.5 lb vs Fierce's 5.3–6.5 lb, so don't claim we're lighter on shipping models — but sub-6 lb Horizon options are coming to market now; reps can tell a weight-driven prospect it's available/imminent rather than a flat "we don't have that." (Confirm the exact model, weight, chambering, and availability with product before quoting.)

## "Walk-away" honesty: when Fierce is genuinely the right call

Be straight with prospects (and with yourself) in these situations — pushing past them costs more in returns/reputation than the sale is worth:

- **A genuinely budget-constrained buyer** for whom the $1,000–$3,000 price gap is the deciding factor, who is risk-tolerant about QC variance, mechanically capable of dealing with a warranty process if needed, and buying from a dealer with a strong return policy. For this buyer, Fierce's value proposition (great when it works, recoverable if it doesn't) is rational.
- **A buyer who needs sub-6 lb and won't wait even weeks.** Horizon's sub-6 lb options are launching now — so for most weight-driven buyers this is no longer an automatic walk-away; check current availability first. Only concede the deal to Fierce (5.3–6.5 lb) if the prospect needs a sub-6 lb rifle *in hand immediately* and our launching SKU can't meet the timeline.
- **A buyer who has already owned a Fierce rifle and had a good experience.** The brand does have a real, satisfied repeat-owner base. Don't try to talk a happy existing Fierce owner out of their own lived experience — the variance argument is about *unknown* risk, and it lands much weaker against someone who already knows which side of the gamble they landed on.
- **A buyer specifically wants the CT Rival XP's titanium-action/full-carbon-barrel spec at its price point**, with eyes open about the documented variance. That spec sheet is genuinely strong and currently undercuts anything Horizon offers at a comparable price.
