# Horizon Sales Playbook — Winning the Competitive Deal

*Phase 2 consolidation. Partisan by design — this is a tool to help Horizon reps win — but **every competitor claim is sourced** to our own research (`/audits/`) and fact-checked. The fastest way to lose a deal is an exaggeration a prospect catches, so the #1 rule of this playbook is: **sell the real gap, never the invented one.***

## The one idea behind everything: sell delivery, not the spec sheet

We researched every rival two ways — how good the rifle is **on paper** (corrected specs) and whether the market believes it **delivers in the field** (owner sentiment). The result is the entire strategy:

> **The delivery ranking nearly inverts the spec ranking. Horizon 7.75 > Seekins 6.85 > Bergara 6.70 > Christensen 5.10 ≈ Fierce 5.05.** The two brands strongest on paper (Fierce, Christensen) are the ones owners trust *least* to honor the promise.

We do **not** win the spec-sheet argument in every matchup — Fierce and Seekins beat us on several, and we're the priciest rifle in ~13 of 14 comparisons. **We win by moving the conversation from "whose PDF is better" to "who actually delivers what they promised, and what happens to you when a rifle doesn't."** That is the ground where every competitor is weak and Horizon is strong.

## Things to lean into — the universal Horizon plays (work against everyone)

**1. Demonstrated delivery, with independent receipts.** Our 0.5 MOA isn't a printed claim — it's independently verified by *non-sponsored* testers: Backfire.tv's blind 20-shot database (Vandal X: 0.739" group / 0.220" mean radius, its "most accurate factory rifle of 2026"), Rifle Magazine (Vandal Prime sub-0.25", "exceeded its ½-inch guarantee"), Petersen's Hunting (Venatic 0.44" avg). *Offer the receipts; ask the prospect to demand the same independent data from the other brand.*

**2. No QC-lottery reputation.** The single most common complaint about **every** established rival is unit-to-unit variance — Fierce "shooter or boat anchor," Christensen "the Taurus of precision rifles" (~25–50% needing a factory rebarrel), Bergara's paint-on-bedding-block / finger-tight-barrel defects, Seekins' bolt-binding and light-strike tail. No comparable pattern surfaced for Horizon in any forum/BBB/press source we reached. *(Honesty caveat below — this rests on lower volume/thinner data; say "no comparable pattern found," never "we've never had a problem.")*

**3. Frictionless ownership when something goes wrong — CONFIRMED, and it's our sharpest wedge.** This is where rivals actively bleed customers: Fierce charges **$200 + shipping just to invoke** its accuracy guarantee, with multi-week-to-month CS silence; Bergara's warranty is a documented **coin-flip** ("told to pound sand"); Christensen carries a **BBB D-** and **2.1/5 "Poor" Trustpilot**; even Seekins' once-great service shows 2025–26 slippage. **Horizon charges no fee for accuracy testing and backs every rifle with a no-questions commitment to make it right** — the premise being that a premium-rifle buyer should be taken care of, period. Say it plainly and contrast it directly with Fierce's $200 fee. **Signature proof point (confirmed): a rifle sent in for service comes back in about two weeks — with a handwritten note from a senior leader and a box of ammo.** That "we take care of you" moment is tangible, memorable, and exactly the kind of thing a volume brand or a components-assembler can't fake. Lead with it.

**4. Buy-once certainty is what the premium actually buys.** We're rarely the cheapest. Reframe: a four-figure rifle buyer isn't purchasing a spec — they're purchasing *not having to gamble* on accuracy or service. Price out the variance, not into your range time.

**5. The 22 Creedmoor pedigree.** Horizon **originated the 22 Creedmoor** and fields the broadest Creedmoor-family menu on its flagship hunters (Venatic Carbon 2 / Venatic 2: 11 chamberings incl. 22 & 25 CM). Use as a brand-authenticity and cartridge-selection wedge — Seekins offers no 22 CM at all. *(Only on the flagship hunters — NOT our single-caliber models; see Guardrails.)*

**6. Make delivery the buying criterion.** Across every discovery call, get the prospect to state that consistency and support matter to them *before* you pitch — then the competitor's own owner data does the closing for you.

## Universal guardrails — claims that will get a rep caught (never say these)

These are contradicted by our **own** research; a prospect who checks will win the argument.

- **Never overstate our delivery data as large-scale proof.** Our 7.75 rests on ~17 sources, Low–Medium confidence, no Reddit/BBB/Trustpilot footprint. Say *"the limited evidence is unanimous and independently sourced,"* never *"thousands of proven owners."*
- **Never claim Horizon has zero QC issues.** We have documented counter-examples (a Sniper's Hide ammo-lot case; a reviewer's "we thought this was a lemon" unit — both resolved well, but real).
- **Never claim we're cheaper.** We usually aren't (15–270% above the named rival). The wedge is *delivered value*, not price.
- **Weight: our *shipping* hunters aren't lighter than Fierce/Christensen (7–7.5 lb vs 5.3–6.5 / 4.8 lb) — but sub-6 lb Horizon options are launching now.** Don't claim current models are lighter; do tell a weight-driven prospect our sub-6 lb option is available/imminent instead of conceding the category. (Confirm exact model, weight, chambering, and availability with product before quoting.)
- **Never imply Horizon ships a per-rifle test target — we don't.** Our accuracy proof is *independent third-party* testing (Backfire.tv, Rifle Magazine, Petersen's) plus the no-fee make-it-right guarantee — not a target in the box. When a rival touts its test target, attack the target's generalizability (one load, 3 shots), not by pretending we ship one too.
- **Never run the chambering-breadth wedge on our single-caliber models** (Vandal Dark II, Villain, Vandal X, Bloodline) — they *lose* on breadth. Flagship hunters only.
- **Never invent a bad BBB score.** Seekins and Fierce are both **A+**. Use forum/Trustpilot evidence where it's actually negative (Christensen), not BBB where it isn't.
- **Never allege lawsuits/FTC action against Christensen** — none is verified; false and a liability risk. Stick to sourced owner sentiment + real BBB/Trustpilot ratings.
- **Never disparage a rival's genuine strength** — Bergara's value, Seekins' reputation, Christensen's brand/weight, Fierce's real accuracy potential are all earned. Concede them; it's what makes the wedge credible.
- **Verify before promising the remaining Horizon specifics** — in-stock/lead times (a real Horizon weakness), exact RMA turnaround days, and exact price deltas: confirm same-day, don't improvise. *(Warranty policy is now settled: no accuracy-testing fee + no-questions make-it-right.)*

## Per-brand quick reference

### 🟡 Seekins — the toughest (respect it: strong on paper *and* in the field, 6.85)
- **Don't deny:** real published prices ($1,895–$2,895), a fired per-rifle test target, TriggerTech Diamond on top models, genuine owner trust.
- **Lead wedge:** 22/25 Creedmoor exclusivity (Seekins offers neither) + our slight, honestly-caveated delivery edge + Seekins' **2025–26 CS slippage** and a documented QC-variance tail (bolt binding, light-strike FTFs).
- **Attack the test target's *generalizability*, not its honesty:** it's one load / 3-shot / their ammo; independent large-N testing (Outdoor Life Havak Slam) averaged ~1.25". Ask both brands for independent multi-group data.
- **One-liner:** *"Seekins is the rival we respect most. We just deliver more consistently, in more chamberings, and don't make you give up the cartridge you want to get the accuracy."*
- **Walk away if:** hard budget under ~$2k; NRL-specific build; committed existing Seekins owner; fast availability is decisive.

### 🟢 Fierce — the best opportunity (wins on paper, LAST on delivery, 5.05)
- **Don't deny:** best corrected spec record, real weight (5.3–6.5 lb) and price edge, genuine accuracy potential, loved carbon barrels; the 4 newest SKUs are *unproven*, not condemned.
- **Lead wedge:** the promise-vs-delivery gap itself + the **$200 fee to invoke the guarantee** + documented "shooter or boat anchor" QC variance and weeks-to-months CS silence.
- **The move:** shift from spec PDFs to *"who's holding the phone when it doesn't group out of the box, and what does it cost you to find out?"*
- **One-liner:** *"Fierce's spec sheet is real. So is the coin-flip on whether the rifle in the box lives up to it — and the $200 you pay to make them check."*
- **Walk away if:** needs sub-6 lb *today*; genuinely budget/risk-tolerant with a good-return-policy dealer; already a happy Fierce owner.

### 🔵 Bergara — the value leader (don't fight on price; qualify the buyer, 6.70)
- **Don't deny:** cheapest in every matchup, near-unconditional value sentiment (8.5), strong reliability/cycling, real sub-MOA with match ammo.
- **Disqualify fast:** a true budget buyer is **not** a Horizon buyer — hand them the B-14 and move on.
- **Lead wedge (premium buyers only):** the *inconsistency* cluster — QC-defect lottery, **warranty coin-flip** (5.5), ammo-sensitive accuracy (1.5–3.5 MOA with the wrong load), 4140 CrMo (not stainless) barrels on the B-14 economy line.
- **One-liner:** *"Bergara is the smartest rifle you can buy for $1–2k. Horizon is what you buy when that much variance — in accuracy or in service — isn't a risk you'll take."*
- **Walk away if:** firm sub-$2k budget, truck/loaner/first rifle, happy to do load development, or a documented good Bergara warranty experience.

### 🟠 Christensen — the brand-love deal (validate taste, reframe on odds, 5.10)
- **Don't deny:** lightest-in-class (Ti FFT 4.8 lb), broadest chamberings (Ridgeline FFT 22+), strongest brand recognition, genuinely liked aesthetics.
- **Lead wedge:** the documented **~25–50% QC "lottery"** (chamber/reamer root cause), **BBB D- + Trustpilot 2.1/5 "Poor,"** and the **3-shot/match-ammo guarantee fine print** vs the 5-shot reality (Outdoor Life 1.14 MOA on the FFT Ti).
- **Tone rule:** never mock the brand — use the *market's own words* ("Taurus of precision rifles"), validate the appeal, then reframe: *you shouldn't have to gamble on a four-figure rifle.*
- **One-liner:** *"When a Christensen is good, it's great — but their own community calls it a lottery. We ship what we promise, every time, and we can show you the receipts."*
- **Walk away if:** pure ultralight is the entire brief; wants the single broadest chambering menu; Mesa-FFT budget with eyes-open lottery tolerance; happy multi-Christensen owner.

## Master discovery questions (work in any competitive deal)

1. *"Is there a specific cartridge you're set on — like 22 or 25 Creedmoor?"* (surfaces our pedigree/exclusivity)
2. *"Have you ever had a rifle that needed a warranty trip to shoot to spec? How did that go?"* (opens QC/CS without naming the rival)
3. *"Are you reading owner forums, or mostly the manufacturer's site and a few reviews?"* (most haven't seen the delivery data)
4. *"Does the accuracy guarantee hold with the ammo you'll actually hunt/compete with, or only a specific match load?"* (sets up ammo-sensitivity + guarantee fine print)
5. *"Do you want a rifle that's interesting to talk about, or one you stop thinking about because it just works?"* (reframes brand cachet as a tradeoff, respectfully)
6. *"When something goes wrong, who do you talk to — and how fast have other owners said that company responds?"* (aims straight at rival CS weakness)
