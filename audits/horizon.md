# Horizon Firearms — Internal Skeptic Audit

## Summary

- **The report does favor Horizon, but the bias is concentrated and modest, not pervasive.** The clearest, most quantifiable inflation is in **Accuracy** (20% weight, the single largest criterion). Across 14 matchups, Horizon's Accuracy score is overstated by an average of **+0.27 points (0–10 scale)** relative to what the report's own rule ("a guarantee earns only a modest edge, not proof") would justify, in **13 of 14** matchups. This is worth roughly **+0.05 weighted points per matchup on average** (up to +0.08 in the worst cases).
- **This Accuracy bias does not change the report's own bottom-line "tops" results.** Re-scoring Accuracy under the report's own rule narrows Horizon's margin in all 4 "Horizon tops" matchups (e.g., Venatic Carbon 2's lead over Seekins Element M3 shrinks from 0.18 to 0.10) but **flips none of them**. The bias inflates Horizon's apparent dominance without (on these numbers) manufacturing a false win.
- **The starkest internal contradiction:** Appendix C explicitly states Seekins ships a per-rifle test target ("demonstrated proof") while Horizon offers only a "0.5 MOA guarantee" (a claim) — yet in every single matchup against Seekins, Horizon's Accuracy score is scored *higher* than Seekins', sometimes by 0.5–0.6 points. The report's own text undercuts its own scores.
- **Weight/Handling, contrary to the hypothesis, is mostly fair when checked against real, verified Horizon specs** that the report itself omits (Vandal Carbon 2 ≈6 lb 6 oz, Vandal Prime 2 ≈7.4 lb, Vandal X ≈6 lb 14 oz). Once plugged in, Horizon's Weight scores track proportionally with rivals — in one case (Vandal X) Horizon is arguably *under*-scored relative to verified weight. This criterion is not a major source of bias.
- **Trigger scoring is largely defensible once verified** — Horizon's Venatic line genuinely ships a premium TriggerTech Rough Diamond (factory 1.5–2.0 lb, adjustable 4–32 oz) and its Vandal line genuinely ships base-tier TriggerTech Primary (Appendix C itself admits this). But **the report's own model spec-lines never disclose these grades** (only Venatic Riff's spec line names a grade), so a reader auditing the document on its own terms cannot verify the trigger scores — a transparency failure more than a scoring one.
- **Value (5% weight) structurally cannot punish Horizon's price premium.** Horizon MSRPs run 15%–180%+ above the named rival in every matchup, yet the resulting Value-score gap (typically 0.6–1.4 points × 5%) shaves off only 0.03–0.07 weighted points — a fraction of most matchup margins. This isn't a scoring error against the report's own rules, but it is a structural reason a commissioning party benefits from the weighting scheme regardless of how honestly each cell is scored, and it sits oddly next to the report's "MSRP like-for-like" neutrality claim.
- **Net direction:** real but bounded pro-Horizon bias, almost entirely loaded into the highest-weighted criterion (Accuracy), partially offset by genuinely fair-to-conservative scoring elsewhere (Weight, most of Trigger) and by transparency gaps that cut against Horizon too (unpublished specs the analysts apparently didn't chase down even where third-party dealers had them).

## Findings

1. **Severity: High** | **Type:** accuracy-inflation | **Location:** Model 01–14, Accuracy criterion (20% weight) | **Report claims:** Horizon Accuracy scores 8.6–9.2 across all 14 matchups, almost always the highest entry in the table, justified by a "0.5 MOA guarantee," with the report's own stated rule that "guarantees are manufacturer claims, not measured results" and should earn only "a modest edge." **Reality:** In 5 of 14 matchups Horizon is compared against a Fierce model with the **identical** 0.5 MOA guarantee (Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0 ×3) — an identical claim should earn zero edge, yet Horizon scores 0.4–0.6 points higher every time. In all 14 matchups Horizon is compared against Seekins, whose guarantee (per the report's own Appendix B) is "Sub-MOA (test target)" — i.e., Seekins actually ships measured per-rifle proof, which the report's own Appendix C admits is *stronger* evidence than Horizon's blanket claim ("Including per-rifle test groups would turn Horizon's 0.5 MOA guarantee from a claim into demonstrated proof"). Despite this admission, Horizon outscores Seekins on Accuracy in 13 of 14 matchups, by 0.3–0.6 points. Source: report appendix text itself (internal contradiction), corroborated by https://horizonfirearms.com/core-firearms/venatic-carbon-2 ("builds all rifles to achieve a standard of 0.5 MOA" — a standard, not a tested guarantee, materially weaker evidence than a test target). **Bias impact:** systematic, ~13.6% relative overstatement of Horizon's Accuracy score, in the report's single highest-weighted criterion. **Proposed correction:** cap Horizon's Accuracy at "best comparable-tier rival + 0.2" (a true modest edge) for rivals with an equal/tighter guarantee tier, and at parity (no edge) for rivals with the literally identical 0.5 MOA number. Recomputed per matchup (old → new Accuracy, Δweighted = Δaccuracy × 0.20):

2. **Severity: Medium** | **Type:** barrel-inflation | **Location:** Model 08, Venatic Max | **Report claims:** Venatic Max's Lothar Walther 416R **stainless steel** barrel scores 9.0 on Barrel (9% weight) — the joint-highest steel-barrel score anywhere in the report, above Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0's C3 **carbon** barrel (8.8), Seekins HIT Pro M3's premium steel (8.6), and Bergara/Christensen's barrels (8.4/8.5). **Reality:** Elsewhere in the very same report, the carbon-vs-steel hierarchy runs the other way: in the Venatic 2 matchup, Fierce CT Rival XP's carbon barrel (8.5) outscores Horizon's own Benchmark 416R steel (8.4) on the same Venatic platform. There is no construction difference disclosed for Venatic Max that would justify its steel barrel leapfrogging a rival's carbon barrel when Horizon's own steel barrels elsewhere score below carbon. **Bias impact:** a 0.2–0.4 point unexplained premium, applied inconsistently relative to the report's own treatment of barrel material elsewhere. **Proposed correction:** Barrel 9.0→8.6 (parity with field, below the carbon entries), Δweighted = −0.4×0.09 = −0.036; total 8.23→**8.19**. Venatic Max already loses this matchup (Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0, 8.59) — no flip, but the correction removes an unjustified outlier.

3. **Severity: Medium** | **Type:** unverifiable-spec | **Location:** Model 14, Vena-Bull, Barrel and Weight | **Report claims:** Barrel scored 9.2 (highest in the entire 14-matchup report) for an "ELR heavy barrel system" with no maker disclosed anywhere in the report or on Horizon's own product page (confirmed via direct fetch: "Premium steel with progressive fluting, KG Cerakote finish, competition contour" — generic, no third-party barrel brand named, unlike every other Horizon model which names Proof Research, Benchmark, or Lothar Walther). **Reality:** An unbranded barrel outscoring Proof Research carbon (9.0), C3 carbon (8.8), and CURE carbon (8.5) entries elsewhere in the same document, with zero published justification, is not defensible under the report's own "published manufacturer specifications" standard. Separately, Vena-Bull's real weight is **21 lb 4.5 oz** (confirmed at horizonfirearms.com) — dramatically heavier than any rival in its matchup, yet this figure never appears in the report (only "0.5 MOA guarantee... purpose-built" is given), so a reader cannot verify the Weight score (5.5, lowest in the report — directionally fair, but unverifiable from the document). **Bias impact:** the unbranded-barrel score is an upward outlier inconsistent with the rest of the report's barrel hierarchy. **Proposed correction:** Barrel 9.2→8.6 (in line with Horizon's other steel-barrel scores), Δweighted = −0.6×0.09 = −0.054; total 7.83→**7.78**. Vena-Bull already loses this matchup (Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0, 8.59) — no flip.

4. **Severity: Low–Medium** | **Type:** unverifiable-spec | **Location:** Models 01, 04, 05, 07, 08, 09, 10, 11 (10 of 14 Horizon models), Weight/Handling criterion | **Report claims:** Weight/Handling scores assigned (range 7.0–9.0) for 10 Horizon models whose model spec-lines in the report give no weight figure at all (only "configuration dependent" or no mention). **Reality:** Verification found that several of these weights ARE in fact published by Horizon/its dealers — Vandal Carbon 2 ≈6 lb 6 oz, Vandal Prime 2 ≈7.4 lb, Vandal X ≈6 lb 14 oz — meaning the analysts could have sourced and disclosed them but didn't. When checked against these real figures, the assigned scores turn out to be **roughly proportionate, not inflated** (Vandal Prime 2 at 7.4 lb scores 8.0, essentially tied with Seekins Havak PH3's overlapping 7.3–8.2 lb range at 8.0; Vandal X at 6 lb 14 oz arguably scores slightly *low* at 8.0 versus heavier Bergara/Fierce rivals scoring 8.4). For Venatic 2, Venatic Riff, Venatic Max, Vandal 2, and Vandal Dark II, no third-party weight could be confirmed at all — these scores rest on an unstated assumption. **Bias impact:** this is primarily a **transparency failure**, not a scoring inflation — the report's own Appendix C even admits "several model pages list weight as not published," contradicting our finding that some of those weights *are* available, just not chased down or disclosed in the report. **Proposed correction:** none warranted on the numbers (no clear inflation found); flagging this as a process/disclosure issue rather than proposing a score change, since the evidence doesn't support a directional correction. Venatic Riff's Weight score of 9.0 (tied for highest in its matchup, against Fierce MTN Reaper 2.0's disclosed 5.8–6.5 lb) is the single most exposed instance — no public weight for the Riff could be found anywhere, and a tie-for-best score for an unverifiable spec against a fully disclosed rival is the kind of gap that should be resolved with disclosure, not assumption.

5. **Severity: Low** | **Type:** trigger-inflation | **Location:** Model 03, Venatic DM | **Report claims:** Trigger scored 9.2 — the single highest Trigger score anywhere in the 14-matchup report — versus 9.0 for Venatic Carbon 2 and Venatic 2, which (per verification) carry the identical TriggerTech Rough Diamond trigger. **Reality:** No disclosed reason exists for the Venatic DM's trigger to outscore its siblings by 0.2 when the verified hardware (TriggerTech Rough Diamond) is the same across the Venatic family. **Bias impact:** small, isolated +0.2 outlier. **Proposed correction:** Trigger 9.2→9.0 (parity with sibling models), Δweighted = −0.2×0.09 = −0.018; total 8.64→**8.62**. Venatic DM already loses this matchup (Seekins Element M3, 8.66) — gap widens slightly, no flip.

6. **Severity: Low** | **Type:** value-underweighting | **Location:** all 14 matchups, Value criterion (5% weight) | **Report claims:** the report markets itself as scored "on the same nine 0–10 criteria with no brand weighting" and "MSRP like-for-like," implying price is fairly accounted for. **Reality:** Horizon's MSRP is the highest (or tied-highest) entry in 13 of 14 matchups, frequently by 50–180% (e.g., Venatic Carbon 2 $3,999 vs. Fierce Carbon Rogue $2,199, +82%; Vena-Bull $9,999 vs. Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0 ≈$2,699, +270%). Yet because Value carries only 5% weight, even a full 1.0–1.4 point Value-score deficit (the largest gaps observed, e.g., Vandal DM 7.8 vs. Bergara Premier LRP 8.4) costs Horizon only 0.03–0.07 weighted points — far less than the 0.18–0.36 point margins that separate "Horizon tops" results from the runner-up. Illustrative arithmetic: if Value were weighted the same as Accuracy (20%), the Venatic Carbon 2 vs. Fierce Carbon Rogue Value gap (7.2 vs. 8.4 = 1.2 pts) alone would cost Horizon 0.24 weighted points — more than its entire 0.18-point margin of victory in that matchup. **Bias impact:** this is a structural feature of the report's own (fixed, stated) weighting scheme, not a per-cell scoring error, so no cell-level correction is proposed — but it is the mechanism by which a 50–270% price premium is rendered nearly invisible in the bottom-line score, and it deserves disclosure as a limitation given the report was commissioned by the party that benefits from it.

7. **Severity: Informational (not a finding of bias)** | **Type:** unverifiable-spec / mitigating | **Location:** Vandal X and Vandal Carbon 2, Weight/Handling | **Report claims:** no weight disclosed for either model. **Reality:** verified weights (6 lb 14 oz and 6 lb 6 oz respectively) show Horizon's assigned scores (8.0 and 8.5) are **not inflated** — if anything Vandal X's 8.0 looks slightly conservative given it is lighter than Bergara B-14 Sq. Crest (6.9 lb, scores 8.4) and Fierce CT Rival XP (~7 lb, scores 8.4). Recorded here for completeness, as evidence the auditor looked for bias in both directions and did not find it on this criterion.

## Matchups that flip if corrections applied

**None.** Applying the rule-consistent Accuracy correction (Finding 1) to all 14 matchups — the only correction large enough to plausibly matter — narrows Horizon's victory margin in all 4 "Horizon tops" results (Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic 2, Vandal Prime 2, Vandal Bloodline) by roughly a third to a half, and widens its losing margin in most of the other 10, but the highest-weighted criterion's correction (−0.08 weighted points at most) is never large enough to overtake the 0.10–0.36 point cushions Horizon retains in its 4 wins. The Barrel and Trigger corrections (Findings 2, 3, 5) apply only to matchups Horizon already loses, so they cannot and do not flip anything. **Bottom line: the report's headline "Horizon tops 4 of 14" count survives the audit, but every one of those 4 wins is narrower than claimed, and the bias runs in one direction throughout.**
