# Christensen Arms — Audit

## Summary

- **Headline error — Christensen MPR weight is wrong by ~2–3 lb.** The report's appendix lists the Modern Precision Rifle at "8–10 lb" and scores its Weight/Handling at 6.9/10 identically across five matchups (Venatic DM, Vandal DM, Venatic Riff, Venatic Max, Vena-Bull). Christensen's own current product page states a 6.9 lb starting weight, corroborated by multiple independent reviews. This is the single largest, most consequential factual error found in this audit.
- **The error is provably inconsistent with how the report treats Bergara.** Bergara's lightweight Premier MgLite (6.6–6.8 lb — almost identical to MPR's real 6.9 lb) scores 8.7 on Weight/Handling in this same report. Christensen MPR, at a verified near-identical weight, scores only 6.9 — a 1.8-point gap for a <0.3 lb difference. That gap is the fingerprint of the spec error, not a defensible scoring judgment.
- Correcting this one spec moves Christensen MPR up to 2nd place in all five of its matchups (from a prior range of 3rd–5th place), and in one matchup (Vandal DM) it overtakes the Horizon model itself.
- **The standard "Ridgeline" used in the Vandal Carbon 2 matchup appears to be a legacy/limited listing today** — Christensen's own site marks it "Legacy Firearm," shows only 4 current chamberings, and prices it at $1,799.99, not the $2,199.99 / ~7-chambering profile the report uses. This cuts both ways and needs Christensen's confirmation; no numeric correction is asserted without it (see Finding 2).
- **Two criteria the brief asked us to scrutinize check out as fair**: Christensen's accuracy guarantee (verified: 3 shots ≤1 MOA at 100 yd, the same tier as Bergara's) is scored consistently with Bergara's own 1.0 MOA scores, and Christensen's hinged-internal-floorplate magazine (not native detachable) is accurately reflected in its below-average Magazine score. The Ridgeline FFT Titanium's broad chambering menu is also already credited near the top of its matchup (9.0, second only to Horizon's own broadest model).
- Arithmetic was independently recomputed for every Christensen column in every matchup; all reported weighted totals tie out correctly (e.g., Venatic DM/MPR: 8.4×.20 + 9.0×.15 + 6.9×.15 + 9.0×.12 + 7.8×.10 + 8.5×.09 + 8.6×.09 + 8.8×.05 + 8.0×.05 = 8.304 ≈ "8.3" as printed). No arithmetic errors were found — every issue identified here is a spec/sourcing issue, not a math error.

## Findings

### 1. Christensen MPR weight is overstated by ~2–3 lb, suppressing its Weight/Handling score across five matchups

**Severity:** High
**Type:** Spec-error, cascading into scoring-inconsistency
**Location:** Appendix A3 (Christensen Arms reference table) and the Weight/Handling row of the Venatic DM (p.06), Vandal DM (p.09), Venatic Riff (p.10), Venatic Max (p.11), and Vena-Bull (p.17) matchups.

**Report claims:** The appendix lists MPR as "Aluminum folding chassis · carbon/steel · TriggerTech · Sub-MOA · AICS · **8–10 lb**." Weight/Handling is scored 6.9/10 for MPR identically in all five matchups where it appears.

**Correct/verified:** Christensen's own current product page states "Starting Weight: 6.9 lbs," independently corroborated by americanhunter.org and other reviews. Even the earlier-generation MPR (2018 NRA American Rifleman review) weighed 7.5 lb unloaded — never the 8–10 lb the report uses. Source: https://christensenarms.com/product/modern-precision-rifle/ ; https://www.americanhunter.org/content/review-christensen-arms-modern-precision-rifle/

**Fairness impact:** This is the report's own internal yardstick turned against it. In the same document, Bergara's Premier MgLite (verified 6.6–6.8 lb — essentially identical to MPR's true 6.9 lb) scores **8.7** on Weight/Handling. Bergara's heavier Premier LRP (9–10 lb — the weight class the report mistakenly assigns to MPR) scores **6.8**. MPR's score of 6.9 tracks the *heavy* Bergara model's weight, not the *light* one it actually matches. The error directly costs Christensen ~1.6–1.8 points on a criterion weighted at 15% of the total score, in five separate matchups.

**Proposed score correction:** Weight/Handling 6.9 → **8.5** (conservative — slightly below Bergara MgLite's 8.7 to account for any heavier magnum/long-barrel configurations within the MPR line), applied in all five matchups. Recomputed weighted totals (criterion weights: Accuracy 20%, Accessories 15%, Weight 15%, Stock 12%, Caliber 10%, Barrel 9%, Trigger 9%, Magazine 5%, Value 5% — all other MPR scores unchanged at 8.4/9.0/—/9.0/7.8/8.5/8.6/8.8/8.0):

| Matchup | Old MPR total | Δ from Weight 6.9→8.5 (+1.6 × 0.15) | New MPR total | Old rank | New rank |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Venatic DM (p.06) | 8.30 | +0.24 | **8.54** | 5th of 5 (last) | 4th of 5 (passes Bergara MgLite 8.49) |
| Vandal DM (p.09) | 8.30 | +0.24 | **8.54** | 4th of 5 | **2nd of 5 — now ahead of Horizon's own Vandal DM (8.32)** |
| Venatic Riff (p.10) | 8.30 | +0.24 | **8.54** | 3rd of 5 | 2nd of 5 (gap to leader Fierce MTN Reaper 2.0, 8.62, narrows to 0.08) |
| Venatic Max (p.11) | 8.30 | +0.24 | **8.54** | 3rd of 5 | 2nd of 5 |
| Vena-Bull (p.17) | 8.30 | +0.24 | **8.54** | 3rd of 5 | 2nd of 5 (gap to leader Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0, 8.59, narrows to 0.05 — a near-tie) |

Arithmetic shown for Vandal DM as a worked example: 8.4×0.20 + 9.0×0.15 + **8.5**×0.15 + 9.0×0.12 + 7.8×0.10 + 8.5×0.09 + 8.6×0.09 + 8.8×0.05 + 8.0×0.05 = 1.68 + 1.35 + 1.275 + 1.08 + 0.78 + 0.765 + 0.774 + 0.44 + 0.40 = **8.544**.

No outright matchup winner changes (Fierce/Seekins models retain top spot in all five), but Christensen's competitive standing was understated in every one of its chassis/precision-class matchups, and in one case the error masked that Christensen MPR — correctly specced — actually beats the Horizon model it was being compared against.

### 2. The "Ridgeline" used in the Vandal Carbon 2 matchup appears to be a stale/legacy listing — price and chambering breadth both need re-verification

**Severity:** Medium
**Type:** Spec-error / unfair-role-match (direction uncertain — flagged, not corrected)
**Location:** Vandal Carbon 2 matchup (p.08) and Appendix A3.

**Report claims:** "Christensen Ridgeline $2,199.99," chamberings "6.5 CM, .270, 6.5/7 PRC, .280 AI, .300 Win/PRC, 28 Nos, 7 RM" (7 representative options), scored Caliber 8.8 (the named category leader of that matchup, ahead of Horizon's own Vandal Carbon 2 at 7.6) and Value 8.4.

**Correct/verified:** Christensen's own current product page for "Ridgeline" labels it a **"Legacy Firearm"** that "cannot be ordered online," lists a price of **$1,799.99**, and shows only **4 chamberings** (6.5 Creedmoor, 6.5 PRC, .308 Win, .300 Win Mag). Market-average pricing data corroborates a lower current price (~$1,763). Source: https://christensenarms.com/product/ridgeline/ ; https://truegunvalue.com/rifle/christensen-arms-ridgeline/price-historical-value

**Fairness impact — cuts both ways, which is why no correction is asserted:**
- If Ridgeline is still genuinely in production and dealer-orderable in the broader chambering set the report describes, then the $2,199.99 price is overstated by ~$400 versus the verified $1,799.99, which would mean Ridgeline's **Value score (8.4) is understated** and should rise — likely into the 8.8–9.0 range that Mesa FFT earns at a comparable real price point.
- If, however, Ridgeline truly is now a narrow, legacy 4-caliber model as Christensen's own site states, then the **Caliber score (8.8, the matchup's category leader) is overstated**, and a closer, currently-marketed analog to the carbon-barreled, $3,399 Vandal Carbon 2 would be the **Ridgeline FFT** (verified 5.3–5.4 lb, 22+ chamberings, $2,399.99) — Christensen's actual current lightweight-carbon flagship, which would score Christensen higher on Weight and Caliber than the model actually used, even after accounting for its higher price reducing the Value score somewhat.

**Resolution (confirmed):** Christensen's site shows the **Ridgeline FFT** as the currently-active lightweight-carbon model; the standard Ridgeline is the legacy listing. Per Horizon's direction, the **Ridgeline FFT** is adopted as the correct current-production analog for the carbon-barreled Vandal Carbon 2 and rescored on its verified specs (5.3–5.4 lb / 22+ chamberings / $2,399.99 / TriggerTech / Sub-MOA / aerograde carbon / FFT carbon stock + brake / hinged-internal mag), calibrated to how this report already scores comparable Christensen carbon models (e.g., Ridgeline FFT Titanium's Caliber 9.0 in the Venatic Carbon 2 matchup):

| Criterion (weight) | Legacy Ridgeline (as used) | Ridgeline FFT (current analog) | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accuracy 20% | 8.3 | 8.3 | Same Sub-MOA tier |
| Accessories 15% | 8.2 | 8.3 | Adds integrated brake |
| Weight 15% | 9.1 | 9.2 | Verified 5.3–5.4 lb |
| Stock 12% | 8.2 | 8.4 | FFT carbon stock upgrade |
| Caliber 10% | 8.8 | 9.0 | Verified 22+ chamberings |
| Barrel 9% | 8.6 | 8.6 | Aerograde carbon-wrapped 416R |
| Trigger 9% | 8.5 | 8.5 | TriggerTech |
| Magazine 5% | 7.3 | 7.3 | Hinged internal floorplate |
| Value 5% | 8.4 | 8.0 | Verified $2,399.99 (vs legacy's lower real price) |
| **Weighted total** | **8.44** | **8.50** | |

The Ridgeline FFT (8.50) is marginally stronger than the legacy listing (8.44). The Vandal Carbon 2 matchup winner is unchanged (Seekins Element M3), but Christensen now sits ahead of Bergara on a verified-current basis (and ahead of Horizon once the consistent-accuracy correction is applied — see consolidated assessment). *(Note: the FFT criterion scores above are an analyst estimate calibrated to this report's own scoring of comparable Christensen carbon models, not figures published by Christensen.)*

### 3. Accuracy-guarantee scoring is fair and internally consistent — no correction needed

**Severity:** Informational
**Type:** Scoring-consistency check (verified fair)
**Location:** All matchups featuring Christensen models.

Christensen's guarantee is confirmed as 3 shots within 1 MOA at 100 yards (https://christensenarmshelp.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/360055265093) — the same guarantee tier as Bergara's published 1.0 MOA guarantee. The report's Christensen accuracy scores (8.2–8.4) sit at or slightly above Bergara's own scores (8.0–8.5) in the same matchups, and properly trail Horizon's and Fierce's tighter 0.5–0.75 MOA guarantees (8.4–9.2 and 8.3–8.6 respectively). This is consistent with the report's own stated rule that "a tighter guarantee earns a modest edge." No fairness issue found here.

---

### 4. Magazine scoring accurately reflects Christensen's hinged-internal-floorplate design — no correction needed

**Severity:** Informational
**Type:** Scoring-consistency check (verified fair)
**Location:** Mesa FFT, Ridgeline, Ridgeline FFT, and Ridgeline FFT Titanium matchups.

All four hunting-rifle models are confirmed via official spec pages to ship with an internal magazine and hinged aluminum floorplate, with AICS compatibility available only via an aftermarket conversion kit — not a native detachable box like most rivals (Seekins, Fierce, Bergara AICS lines). The below-average Magazine scores (~7.3) the report assigns to these models are an accurate reflection of that real spec difference, not an unfair penalty. The MPR, which genuinely has a native AICS-compatible detachable magazine, correctly scores 8.8 — the highest Magazine score among the Christensen lineup. No correction warranted.

---

### 5. Chambering-breadth crediting is mostly fair, and in one case generous, wherever a current (non-legacy) model is used

**Severity:** Informational
**Type:** Scoring-consistency check (verified mostly fair)
**Location:** Venatic Carbon 2 matchup (Ridgeline FFT Titanium, p.04); Mesa FFT matchups (pp.05, 07, 12, 13, 14, 15).

Ridgeline FFT Titanium's Caliber score of 9.0 — second only to Horizon's own broadest-chambering model in that matchup — is well-supported by its verified 13-chambering catalog. Mesa FFT's consistent 8.6 Caliber score, frequently named the matchup's category leader against single-caliber Horizon models (e.g., Vandal X, Vandal Dark II, Villain), is reasonably supported by its verified ~9-chambering catalog. Taken together with the Ridgeline FFT figures in Finding 2, this indicates the report does generally credit Christensen's chambering breadth where current model data is used correctly — the gap identified in this audit is concentrated in the single (likely legacy) Ridgeline listing, not a systemic pattern.

## Net effect on rankings

Applying Finding 1 (the only finding with a sufficiently confirmed factual basis to recompute) changes Christensen MPR's standing in all five of its matchups:

- **Venatic DM, Venatic Riff, Venatic Max, Vena-Bull:** Christensen MPR moves up one rank position in each (to 4th, 2nd, 2nd, and 2nd of 5, respectively); no matchup winner changes, but MPR's gap to the leader narrows sharply — in Vena-Bull, from 0.29 to a near-tie of 0.05.
- **Vandal DM:** Christensen MPR overtakes the Horizon model itself (8.54 vs. 8.32), moving from 4th to 2nd place and pushing Horizon's Vandal DM from 3rd to 4th. The matchup winner (Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0) is unchanged, but the report's framing of this matchup — which currently reads as a clean Horizon #2 finish — should reflect that a correctly-specced Christensen MPR outperforms the Horizon model.

Finding 2 is now resolved by substituting the current-production **Ridgeline FFT** (8.50) for the legacy Ridgeline (8.44) in the Vandal Carbon 2 matchup. Christensen moves from 5th to 4th (ahead of Bergara) under the facts-only correction, and — once the consistent-accuracy correction lowers Horizon's Vandal Carbon 2 to 8.45 — Christensen's 8.50 also moves **ahead of the Horizon model**. The matchup's named winner (Seekins Element M3, 8.69 corrected) does not change.

No matchup's named "Top-scoring rifle" (the headline result for any of the 14 Horizon models) changes as a result of this audit's corrections.
