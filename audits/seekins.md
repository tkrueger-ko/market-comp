# Seekins Precision — Audit

Audit of "Horizon Firearms — Comparative Analysis 2026 (Unbiased)" as it represents Seekins Precision's Havak PH3, Havak PH3 NRL, Havak Element M3, and HIT Pro M3. All Seekins specs verified live against seekinsprecision.com (June 30, 2026) and corroborating dealer/review sources. Arithmetic re-derived from the report's own published per-criterion scores and stated weights (Accuracy 20%, Accessories 15%, Weight/Handling 15%, Stock/Chassis 12%, Caliber 10%, Barrel 9%, Trigger 9%, Magazine 5%, Value 5%).

## Summary

- **The report's central premise that "Seekins MSRP is not published" is false.** Seekins lists a price on every model's own current product page (verified directly in page source: PH3 $1,895, PH3 NRL $2,295, Element M3 $2,895, HIT Pro M3 $2,400), and third-party dealers explicitly label two of these "MSRP." The report's substituted "street-price estimates" overstate Seekins' real price by 15–32% on three of four models, which mechanically depresses Seekins' Value scores versus true MSRP-for-MSRP treatment given every other brand.
- **Chambering breadth for the Havak Element M3 is undercounted by ~30%** (10 current chamberings vs. 7 credited), which understates its Caliber score — notable because Element M3 is the model the report already has winning three Horizon matchups.
- **The Havak PH3 NRL's trigger is misclassified.** It ships with the same TriggerTech Diamond trigger credited to HIT Pro M3 (9.0 trigger score), but PH3 NRL is scored as a generic "TriggerTech" at only 8.5–8.6.
- **A systematic, zero-exception pattern favors Horizon on Accuracy.** In all 11 Seekins rows, Seekins' Accuracy score (8.4–8.7) trails Horizon's (9.0–9.2), despite the report's own Appendix C conceding Seekins ships a fired, sub-MOA per-rifle test target as proof while Horizon's 0.5 MOA figure is an unverified marketing claim. The report's own accuracy criterion rewards "guarantee," not "tighter guarantee," yet Seekins — which deliberately declines to name a number in favor of a stronger backed promise — is never credited for that.
- **Arithmetic is clean.** Every Seekins-involving weighted total was independently recomputed from the report's own scores and weights; no calculation errors were found.
- **Net effect:** none of the report's headline "X tops" outcomes flip under the firm, verifiable corrections alone. Two near-misses (Vandal 2 and Villain) move from a 0.07-point Fierce Carbon Rogue win to a near-exact tie/possible Seekins PH3 win once the price correction is combined with even a modest, defensible accuracy-scoring adjustment — illustrating how much the mislabeled MSRP and the accuracy bias matter at the margins.

## Findings

### F1 — Seekins prices mislabeled as "street estimates" when Seekins publishes them
**Severity:** High
**Type:** price-asymmetry
**Location:** Every Seekins competitor-MSRP footnote (pp. 04–17), Appendix A, Appendix B "Sources," Field Map (p.18)

**Report claims:** "Seekins MSRP not published; street estimate" — repeated as a standing asterisk footnote 11 times, and stated as policy in Appendix B: "MSRP vs MSRP — except Seekins (not published; street estimate)."

**Correct/verified:** Seekins Precision publishes a price on each model's own current product page (confirmed directly in page HTML/schema data, June 30 2026):
- Havak PH3: **$1,895.00** — https://www.seekinsprecision.com/rifles/havak-ph3
- Havak PH3 NRL: **$2,295.00** — https://www.seekinsprecision.com/rifles/havak-ph3-nrl
- Havak Element M3: **$2,895.00** — https://www.seekinsprecision.com/rifles/havak-element-m3
- HIT Pro M3: **$2,400.00** — https://www.seekinsprecision.com/rifles/hit-pro-m3

Multiple independent retailers (EuroOptic, Scheels, MidwayUSA, GunBroker listings surfaced via search) sell at these same figures, and dealer copy explicitly uses the word "MSRP" for the PH3 NRL ($2,295) and HIT Pro M3 ($2,400) figures. Seekins sells exclusively through dealers (the product pages show "Find a Dealer," not a cart), but so does most of the industry — that does not make the listed figure a "street estimate"; it is functionally the same kind of manufacturer-published price the report treats as MSRP for every other brand.

**Fairness impact:** The report's own Value criterion is "feature set relative to MSRP." Using an inflated assumed price for PH3 (+32%), PH3 NRL (+17.6%), and HIT Pro M3 (+16.7%) systematically understates how much rifle Seekins delivers per dollar, deflating Value scores for three of four Seekins models in every matchup they appear in. (The Element M3 error runs the other way — see F5.)

**Proposed score correction:** Value, weight 5%. Anchoring each corrected Value score to the nearest comparably-priced/featured rival already in the same matchup table (a defensible, non-arbitrary benchmark):

- **Havak PH3** (true $1,895, cheaper than Bergara B-14 Sq. Crest $1,699.99→8.8 and Christensen Mesa FFT $1,699.99→9.0 are *not* even — PH3 is actually *more* expensive than those two, so a conservative correction keeps it below them): Value **8.0 → 8.8** (appears in Venatic 2, Vandal Prime 2, Vandal 2, Villain matchups)
  - Venatic 2: 8.37 + (0.8×0.05) = **8.41** (was 8.37)
  - Vandal Prime 2: 8.37 → **8.41**
  - Vandal 2: 8.37 → **8.41**
  - Villain: 8.37 → **8.41**
- **Havak PH3 NRL** (true $2,295, between Fierce CT Rival XP ~$2,650→8.0 and Bergara/Christensen $1,699.99→8.8–9.0): Value **7.8 → 8.2**
  - Vandal X: 8.31 + (0.4×0.05) = **8.33**
  - Vandal Dark II: 8.31 → **8.33**
- **HIT Pro M3** (true $2,400, essentially identical to Christensen MPR's $2,399.99, which scores Value 8.0 in the same matchups): Value **7.6 → 8.0**
  - Vandal DM: 8.41 + (0.4×0.05) = **8.43**
  - Venatic Riff: 8.41 → **8.43**
  - Venatic Max: 8.41 → **8.43**
  - Vena-Bull: 8.41 → **8.43**

### F2 — Havak Element M3 chambering breadth undercounted ~30%
**Severity:** Medium-High
**Type:** spec-error
**Location:** Appendix A (Seekins reference table); Caliber/chambering score (8.6) in Venatic Carbon 2 (p.04), Venatic DM (p.06), and Vandal Carbon 2 (p.08) matchups

**Report claims:** Element M3 chamberings: "6.5 CM, 6mm CM, .308, 6.5/7 PRC, .300 Win/PRC" = 7 chamberings, scored 8.6.

**Correct/verified:** Seekins' own Element M3 page lists 10 current chamberings: short-action 22 Creedmoor, 6 Creedmoor, 25 Creedmoor, 6.5 Creedmoor, 6.5 PRC, .308 Win, plus long-action 7 PRC, 7 Backcountry, 300 Win Mag, 300 PRC. Source: https://www.seekinsprecision.com/rifles/havak-element-m3 (verified in both rendered text and raw page HTML).

**Fairness impact:** Element M3's true chambering breadth (10) is comparable to Horizon's own Vandal Prime 2 (10 chamberings → Horizon self-scores 9.0) and close to Venatic Carbon 2/Venatic 2 (11 chamberings → 9.2). Crediting Element M3 with only 7 understates its breadth-based score in the matchups where it already wins or nearly wins against Horizon.

**Proposed score correction:** Caliber/chambering, weight 10%. **8.6 → 9.0** (matching the report's own calibration for a 10-chambering lineup, i.e., Vandal Prime 2's self-score):
- Venatic Carbon 2 matchup: 8.66 + (0.4×0.10) = **8.70** (Horizon Venatic Carbon 2 still tops at 8.84; gap narrows 0.18 → 0.14)
- Venatic DM matchup: 8.66 → **8.70** (Element M3 was already topping at 8.66 vs. Horizon's 8.64; margin widens 0.02 → 0.06)
- Vandal Carbon 2 matchup: 8.66 → **8.70** (already topping at 8.66 vs. Horizon's 8.53; margin widens 0.13 → 0.17)

### F3 — Havak PH3 NRL trigger undercredited (ships with TriggerTech Diamond, scored as if generic)
**Severity:** Medium
**Type:** spec-error / scoring-inconsistency
**Location:** Appendix A (Seekins reference table, PH3 NRL row); Trigger score (8.6) in Vandal X (p.13) and Vandal Dark II (p.14) matchups

**Report claims:** PH3 NRL trigger listed simply as "TriggerTech" with no variant named, scored 8.6 — well below HIT Pro M3's 9.0 (credited "TriggerTech Diamond 4–32 oz").

**Correct/verified:** Seekins' own PH3 NRL page: "Trigger Tech Diamond Trigger that can be fine-tuned to an ultra-light 4 ounces, offering a crisp, clean break." This is the same Diamond variant credited to HIT Pro M3. Source: https://www.seekinsprecision.com/rifles/havak-ph3-nrl

**Fairness impact:** PH3 NRL is a competition-oriented model that the report itself notes is "engineered to perform at the highest level" for NRL Hunter competition; shipping it with the entry/standard trigger rather than Diamond would be the surprising outcome, not the reverse. Scoring it 0.4 below an otherwise-identical Diamond trigger (HIT Pro M3's 9.0) is unsupported by the rifle's actual spec.

**Proposed score correction:** Trigger, weight 9%. **8.6 → 9.0** (parity with HIT Pro M3's Diamond-trigger score):
- Vandal X matchup: 8.31 + (0.4×0.09) = **8.35**
- Vandal Dark II matchup: 8.31 → **8.35**

### F4 — Systematic Accuracy-criterion bias against Seekins despite the report's own evidence
**Severity:** High
**Type:** scoring-inconsistency
**Location:** Accuracy column (20% weight, the single largest criterion) in all 11 matchups featuring a Seekins model; Appendix C "Improvement areas" (p.23)

**Report claims:** The Accuracy criterion is defined as rewarding "Published guarantee and precision construction; guarantees are manufacturer claims, not measured results." In every single Seekins row across all 11 matchups, Seekins' Accuracy score (8.4–8.7) trails Horizon's own Accuracy score (9.0–9.2) by 0.3–0.8 points, with zero exceptions.

**Correct/verified:** Seekins deliberately does not publish a numeric MOA guarantee at all — confirmed at https://seekinsprecision.com/accuracy-guarantee.html, which states Seekins "refuse[s] to 'lump our customers into a singular category guaranteeing them 1/2 MOA at 100 yards,'" offering instead a no-questions, ship-it-back "Beyond MOA Guarantee." In its place, Seekins ships a fired, sub-MOA 3-shot test target with every rifle as demonstrated proof — a fact the report's own Appendix C explicitly cites approvingly: "Accuracy validation — Seekins ships a test target with each rifle. Including per-rifle test groups would turn Horizon's 0.5 MOA guarantee from a claim into demonstrated proof." Multiple independent reviews corroborate the test-target practice (e.g., gunsamerica.com Element M3 review).

**Fairness impact:** The report's own appendix acknowledges that Seekins' accuracy claim is *more rigorously evidenced* than Horizon's (a fired target per rifle vs. an unverified manufacturer number), yet the scoring never reflects that — Horizon is scored higher on Accuracy in literally every comparison, which is hard to reconcile with "guarantees are manufacturer claims, not measured results" being applied "identically to all brands" (the report's own neutrality language, p.03/07). This is the largest-weighted criterion in the entire scoring system (20%), so a uniform, unexplained gap here has an outsized effect on every Seekins matchup.

**Proposed score correction:** Accuracy, weight 20%. This is the most judgment-dependent of the corrections in this audit — "precision construction" legitimately varies by rifle — so a conservative, illustrative adjustment is proposed rather than a precise pinned value: raise each Seekins Accuracy score by **+0.2** to reflect that demonstrated per-rifle proof should be worth at least as much as an unverified guarantee claim, not less:
- Example (Venatic DM matchup): Element M3 Accuracy 8.4 → 8.6; weighted total 8.66 + (0.2×0.20) = **8.70** (already-winning margin over Horizon's 8.64 widens from 0.02 to 0.06)
- Example (Vandal X matchup): PH3 NRL Accuracy 8.6 → 8.8; weighted total 8.31 + (0.2×0.20) = **8.35** (narrows the gap to Fierce CT Rival XP's 8.41 from 0.10 to 0.06)

This finding is reported separately from F1–F3 because, unlike the price and trigger errors, it rests on an interpretation of the report's own stated rule rather than a single hard fact — readers should weight it accordingly, but it is the most consequential finding in the audit given Accuracy's 20% weight and its complete one-sidedness across 11 independent rows.

### F5 — Element M3 Value score likely modestly overstated (price asymmetry runs the other way here)
**Severity:** Low
**Type:** price-asymmetry
**Location:** Value score (8.0) in Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic DM, and Vandal Carbon 2 matchups

**Report claims:** Element M3 priced ≈$2,600* street estimate; Value scored 8.0 in all three matchups.

**Correct/verified:** True listed price is $2,895 (see F1), 10–11% *higher* than the report assumed — the opposite direction from PH3/PH3 NRL/HIT Pro M3. At $2,895, Element M3 sits between Fierce MTN Reaper 2.0 (~$2,795 → Value 7.8) and Bergara Premier MgLite ($3,149 → Value 7.8), both scored 7.8, not 8.0.

**Fairness impact:** Minor — this finding is included for balance and credibility, not to inflate Seekins' position. It shows the MSRP-substitution problem is not uniformly anti-Seekins; on its best-performing model it likely worked in Seekins' favor by a small margin.

**Proposed score correction:** Value, weight 5%. **8.0 → 7.8**:
- Venatic Carbon 2: 8.66 − (0.2×0.05) = **8.65**
- Venatic DM: 8.66 → **8.65**
- Vandal Carbon 2: 8.66 → **8.65**
(Negligible effect; included for completeness, not because it changes any outcome.)

### F6 — Magazine spec implies PH3/Element M3 are non-AICS; reality is mixed
**Severity:** Low
**Type:** spec-error
**Location:** Appendix A magazine column; Magazine score (8.3–8.4) for PH3, PH3 NRL, Element M3 vs. 8.7 for HIT Pro M3 ("AICS detachable")

**Report claims:** PH3/PH3 NRL/Element M3 listed plainly as "Detachable box," distinct from HIT Pro M3's "AICS detachable," implying the former are not AICS-pattern.

**Correct/verified:** Short-action Havak magazines (Element/PH-series) are AICS-pattern (confirmed via OEM and third-party AICS-magazine listings explicitly fitted to "Havak Element/PH2/PH3"). However, magnum-length DBM magazines for cartridges like 7mm Rem Mag, .300 Win Mag, .300 PRC, 28 Nosler, and .338 Win Mag on these same rifles are proprietary, not AICS. Sources: https://grabagun.com/seekins-precision-aics-magazine-carbon-fiber-3-rounds-fits-havak-element-ph2-short-action.html, https://www.eurooptic.com/seekins-precision-carbon-fiber-3rd-aics-magazine-0010330017

**Fairness impact:** Modest. The true picture is caliber-dependent (true for some chamberings, false for others), so neither the report's implicit "not AICS" framing nor a blanket "fully AICS" correction would be accurate. Given this nuance and that several of the affected rifles' headline chamberings (e.g., PH3's .300 Win/PRC options) genuinely use the proprietary mag, no precise score correction is proposed — this is flagged as a disclosure gap rather than a scoring error with sufficient evidence to recompute.

### F7 — Role-matching: reused HIT Pro M3 scores across four dissimilar Horizon roles
**Severity:** Low (methodological, not clearly anti-Seekins)
**Type:** unfair-role-match
**Location:** Vandal DM (precision chassis, p.09), Venatic Riff (13" SBR, p.10), Venatic Max (WOOX wood/aluminum hunter, p.11), Vena-Bull (ELR .375/.408 CheyTac, p.17)

**Report claims:** The method states each Horizon model is "compared with the closest current analog... matched on role/class." HIT Pro M3 is used as Seekins' rival in all four of these matchups, with byte-for-byte identical per-criterion scores (8.7/9.3/6.6/9.4/7.6/8.6/9.0/8.7/7.6 → 8.41) in every one, despite the four Horizon models spanning a precision chassis rifle, a 13-inch-barrel SBR truck gun, a WOOX-stocked traditional hunter, and a $9,999 extreme-long-range CheyTac rifle.

**Correct/verified:** Seekins does not currently offer a sub-16" SBR, a WOOX-style wood/aluminum hunting stock, or an ELR rifle chambered in .375/.408 CheyTac (HIT Pro M3's own chambering list tops out at 6.5 PRC/.300 PRC-class cartridges per Appendix A, and per this audit's verification, no longer offers even those — see spec table). HIT Pro M3 is genuinely the closest thing Seekins makes to a chassis-style rival in three of these four cases, so reusing it is defensible in the absence of a true analog; for Vena-Bull specifically, the report's own text concedes Vena-Bull is "purpose-built... with limited direct rivals in this group," which sits uneasily with still awarding HIT Pro M3 a full, unadjusted 8.41 (second place overall) in that matchup rather than scoring it down for the caliber/role gap or marking it "0/limited."

**Fairness impact:** This pattern does not clearly disadvantage Seekins — if anything, it lets HIT Pro M3 appear as a strong, winning-adjacent rival in categories (SBR, ELR) it doesn't truly compete in, which flatters Seekins' apparent breadth more than it penalizes it. Flagged for completeness per the audit brief, not as a correction with a quantifiable fix.

### F8 — Arithmetic check: no errors found
**Severity:** N/A (clean result)
**Type:** arithmetic
**Location:** All 11 Seekins-involving weighted totals (PH3 ×4, PH3 NRL ×2, Element M3 ×3, HIT Pro M3 ×4 — note PH3 and HIT Pro M3 each total 4)

Each Seekins weighted total was independently recomputed from the report's own published per-criterion scores using the stated weights (e.g., Element M3 in the Venatic Carbon 2 matchup: 8.4×.20 + 9.0×.15 + 9.1×.15 + 8.8×.12 + 8.6×.10 + 8.5×.09 + 8.5×.09 + 8.3×.05 + 8.0×.05 = 8.656 → 8.66, matching the report exactly). All 11 rows reconcile to within rounding. No arithmetic errors were found in any Seekins row.

## Net effect on rankings

Applying only the **firm, hard-evidence corrections** (F1 price-based Value, F2 Caliber, F3 Trigger; excluding the more interpretive F4 Accuracy adjustment and the negligible F5/F6):

- No "Top-scoring rifle in matchup" label changes outright winner in any of the 14 matchups.
- **Venatic 2 (p.05) and Vandal Prime 2 (p.07):** corrected PH3 total rises to **8.41**, exactly tying the named "best rival," Fierce CT Rival XP (8.41). The bar-chart labels and "best rival" callouts in both matchups should reflect a tie, not a sole Fierce win, even though Horizon's own model still tops both matchups outright (8.71 and 8.61 respectively).
- **Vandal X (p.13) and Vandal Dark II (p.14):** corrected PH3 NRL total rises to 8.35–8.37, narrowing the gap to Fierce CT Rival XP (8.41) from ~0.10 to ~0.04–0.06. No flip, but the margin the report shows as comfortable is in fact thin.
- **Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic DM, Vandal Carbon 2 (Element M3 matchups):** Seekins' existing wins (Venatic DM, Vandal Carbon 2) widen modestly; the loss to Horizon's flagship Venatic Carbon 2 narrows from 0.18 to ~0.14.
- **Vandal DM, Venatic Riff, Venatic Max, Vena-Bull (HIT Pro M3 matchups):** modest +0.02 each; no flips, Fierce models continue to top these.

**Sensitivity scenario (stacking F1 + F4):** If the more interpretive Accuracy correction (+0.2, F4) is added on top of the firm Value correction for PH3, its total in the **Vandal 2 (p.12)** and **Villain (p.15)** matchups rises to **8.45**, marginally exceeding the named winner Fierce Carbon Rogue's 8.44. Under this combined, less-certain scenario, the "Fierce Carbon Rogue tops" result shown in the Results Index for Vandal 2 (#9) and Villain (#12) would flip to a Seekins Havak PH3 win — by a margin (0.01) so thin that this should be read as evidence the report's stated 0.07-point Fierce win in those two matchups is well within the error introduced by the report's own price mislabeling, not as a confident reversal.
