# Consolidated Unbiased Assessment — Horizon Comparative Analysis 2026

**Subject:** `Horizon_Comparative_Analysis_UNBIASED_2026.html` — a Horizon-commissioned, 23-page comparison scoring 14 Horizon bolt-action rifles against role-matched rivals from Seekins Precision, Fierce Firearms, Bergara, and Christensen Arms on nine weighted criteria.

**Method:** Five independent Sonnet auditors were run — one per competitor and one internal skeptic for Horizon — each web-verifying every spec/price/chambering against live 2026 manufacturer sources and testing the report against *its own* stated scoring rules. This document reconciles their findings, resolves conflicts where corrections stack, and recomputes the contested matchups under one consistent ruleset. Underlying audits: [`seekins.md`](seekins.md), [`fierce.md`](fierce.md), [`bergara.md`](bergara.md), [`christensen.md`](christensen.md), [`horizon.md`](horizon.md).

---

## 1. Bottom-line verdict

**The report is more honest than most commissioned marketing, but it is not neutral. Its scoring is materially distorted by (a) a handful of verified factual errors in competitor spec sheets, and (b) one systematic, single-direction scoring bias in the heaviest-weighted criterion (Accuracy).** Crucially, the bias and the errors **both run the same way** — they flatter Horizon and suppress rivals — which is the pattern you would expect from a thumb on the scale, even if no single error is individually damning.

- The report's central claim ("Horizon takes the top score in **4 of 14**") **survives a strict facts-only correction**, but every one of those four wins narrows, and the competitive podium reshuffles substantially in Horizon's disfavor.
- When the report's *own* Accuracy rule ("a guarantee is a claim, not a measurement; it earns only a modest edge") is applied **consistently to Horizon as well as rivals**, **one of the four Horizon wins flips** (Vandal Prime 2 → Fierce) and a second (Venatic 2) becomes a near-tie.
- Three of the four competitor audits independently surfaced the **same** Accuracy inequity, and — tellingly — our **own** internal skeptic agent surfaced it too. That convergence is the single most important result of this exercise.

**Net:** the document is usable as a structured spec comparison, but its headline framing of Horizon's dominance is overstated. The corrections below are what an genuinely unbiased version would show.

## 2. Cross-cutting findings (themes that appear across multiple audits)

### 2.1 Accuracy (20% weight) systematically favors Horizon — flagged by 4 of 5 audits
This is the most consequential finding and the only one corroborated from every direction.

- Horizon's 0.5 MOA models score **9.0–9.2** on Accuracy in 13 of 14 matchups — almost always the table-topping value.
- **Fierce** sells multiple rifles with the **identical 0.5 MOA guarantee** (Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0, and — once reclassified, see 2.3 — CT Rival XP), yet they are capped at **8.6** in every matchup. An identical claim earns Horizon +0.4 to +0.6 it does not earn anyone else.
- **Seekins** ships a **fired, per-rifle sub-MOA test target** — demonstrated proof, which the report's *own* Appendix C concedes is *stronger* evidence than Horizon's unverified claim — yet Seekins is scored *below* Horizon on Accuracy in all 11 of its matchups.
- The report's own rule says guarantees "are manufacturer claims, not measured results" and earn only "a modest edge." Horizon's Accuracy scores violate that rule in its own favor.
- **Counter-check (fairness in both directions):** the Bergara and Christensen audits found the Accuracy *spread* applied to *them* (1.0-MOA tier scored ~8.2–8.5) is largely defensible once barrel construction is accounted for — so this is specifically a Horizon-vs-equal-or-better-guarantee problem, not a blanket miscalibration. Our own Horizon skeptic quantified the inflation at **~+0.27 Accuracy points / +0.05 weighted points per matchup**, one-directional.

### 2.2 Value is weighted at only 5% — structurally muting price, in a report commissioned by the most expensive brand
Raised independently by the Bergara and Horizon audits.
- Horizon's MSRP is the highest or tied-highest in **13 of 14** matchups, by margins of **15% to 270%** (Vena-Bull at $9,999 is +270% over its named rival).
- Because Value carries only 5% weight, even a full 1.0–1.4-point Value deficit costs Horizon only **0.03–0.07** weighted points — smaller than the victory margins it's credited with.
- This is a **disclosed, uniformly-applied** methodology choice, *not* a rule violation. But it is the mechanism by which a premium-priced commissioning party benefits regardless of how each cell is scored, and it sits awkwardly beside the report's "MSRP like-for-like" neutrality claim. Bergara — the cheapest rifle in *every* matchup it appears in, often by $500–$2,800 — **never wins a single matchup** despite winning the Value criterion outright in most of them.
- A still-conservative reweight (Value 10%, Accuracy 15%) would not, by itself, flip outcomes — but it would honestly represent the price reality the current 5% hides.

### 2.4 Role-matching shortcuts and a sourcing defect
- The same competitor scorecard is **reused verbatim** across dissimilar Horizon roles (e.g., Seekins HIT Pro M3 and Bergara Premier LRP each appear with byte-identical scores against a precision chassis rifle, a 13″ SBR, a wood-stocked hunter, and a $9,999 CheyTac ELR rifle). This doesn't clearly disadvantage those brands, but it undercuts the "matched on role/class" claim.
- **Vena-Bull ($9,999 ELR)** has no genuine peer in the comparison set; the report half-admits this. That "Fierce tops" result is not load-bearing.
- The report cites **`fiercefirearms.com`** as its Fierce source; that domain **does not resolve**. The real site is `fiercearms.com`. A reader cannot verify Fierce specs from the cited source.

## 3. Reconciled corrected rankings

Each agent computed its correction in isolation; the tables below apply **all** verified corrections **simultaneously** (so stacking is handled correctly) under one consistent rule. Two scenarios are shown because the corrections fall into two evidentiary tiers.

**Scenario A — verified factual corrections only** (objective; no interpretation). Applies the spec/price/chambering fixes in §2.3 to all brands at once, including the ones that cut against competitors. Leaves the Accuracy criterion exactly as the report scored it.

**Scenario B — Scenario A + consistent Accuracy rule.** Additionally removes Horizon's unearned Accuracy edge over rivals with an equal-or-better guarantee, applying the report's *own* "modest edge" rule once (Horizon's 0.5-MOA scores brought to parity; the gap is closed a single time, not double-counted).

Only the five genuinely contested matchups are recomputed here; the other nine are not close enough for any correction to change. Full transcription reproduces the report's printed totals exactly before correction.

| Matchup | Reported winner & margin | Scenario A (facts only) | Scenario B (+ consistent accuracy) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Venatic 2** | Horizon **8.71** (+0.30) | Horizon **8.71** holds; Fierce CT Rival XP rises to 8.57 (+0.14) | Horizon **8.63** holds but margin cut to **+0.06** |
| **Venatic DM** | Seekins Element M3 **8.66** (Horizon #2 by 0.01) | Seekins **8.69** tops; Horizon 8.64 & Fierce 8.64 **tie for #2**; Christensen MPR leaps to 8.54 | Seekins **8.69** tops; Horizon **drops to #3** behind Fierce (8.64) |
| **Vandal Prime 2** | Horizon **8.61** (+0.20) | Horizon **8.61** holds; Fierce CT Rival XP rises to 8.57 (+0.04, near-tie) | **FLIPS → Fierce CT Rival XP 8.57** beats Horizon 8.53 |
| **Vandal Carbon 2** | Seekins Element M3 **8.66** (Horizon #3) | Seekins **8.69** tops; Fierce Rage LR 8.60; Horizon #3 at 8.53 | Seekins **8.69** tops; Horizon **drops to #4** (8.45), behind Bergara |
| **Vandal DM** | Fierce Reaper H-TAC **8.59** (Horizon #3) | Fierce **8.63** tops; **Christensen MPR jumps to #2 (8.54)**, ahead of Horizon (8.32) | Fierce **8.63** tops; Christensen #2; Horizon #4 at 8.30 |

**Reading the result:**
- **Facts-only (Scenario A):** no outright winner flips, so the report's "Horizon tops 4 of 14" headline technically survives — **but** Horizon falls a place in Vandal DM (now behind a correctly-weighed Christensen MPR), its two reported wins shrink, and Seekins/Fierce/Christensen all climb. The "honest winner" framing is directionally right in the aggregate while overstating Horizon's standing in the details.
- **Consistent-accuracy (Scenario B):** **Vandal Prime 2 flips to Fierce**, dropping Horizon to **3 clear wins** (Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic 2, Vandal Bloodline) plus contested Vandal Prime 2 — and Venatic 2 becomes a 0.06 near-tie. Horizon's other two wins not recomputed here hold per the underlying audits: **Venatic Carbon 2**'s lead over Seekins narrows from 0.18 to ~0.10, and **Vandal Bloodline** (450 Bushmaster, only Christensen/Bergara rivals) is uncontested.

Both scenarios agree on the qualitative conclusion: **Horizon's lead is real in its premium broad-chambering hunters (Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic 2) and genuinely thin-to-absent everywhere else.**

## 4. Per-brand summary

- **Seekins Precision** — The most materially mishandled brand. The false "MSRP not published" premise inflates Seekins prices 16–32% and the Accuracy criterion never credits its per-rifle tested proof. Correcting only hard facts moves two matchups to ties and lifts Element M3's existing wins; Seekins is the strongest competitor in the value-adjusted picture.
- **Fierce Firearms** — Best head-to-head performer once errors are fixed. The CT Rival XP misclassification (steel→carbon/Ti) and ~½ chambering undercounts suppressed a brand that, corrected, tops the most matchups of any competitor and flips Vandal Prime 2 under the consistent accuracy rule. (One error favored Fierce — understated MTN Reaper MSRP — and was corrected against it.)
- **Christensen Arms** — One large error (MPR weight 8–10 lb vs real 6.9 lb) cost it ~1.6 points in five matchups; corrected, MPR rises to #2 in all five and beats Horizon's own Vandal DM. Its guarantee and magazine scoring were verified **fair**. One unresolved item: the "Ridgeline" analog appears to be a legacy listing — publisher should re-verify or substitute Ridgeline FFT.
- **Bergara** — Mostly **accurately** represented; most specs check out. Its poor standing is not chiefly an error problem — it is the **5% Value weight** structurally erasing the largest, most verifiable price advantage in the field. One real fix: Premier Cima Pro chambering breadth undercounted 4→9. Net price corrections actually run **both** ways.
- **Horizon (internal skeptic)** — Confirms the report favors Horizon, but bounded and concentrated: ~+0.27 Accuracy points/matchup, plus isolated Barrel outliers (Venatic Max steel barrel and Vena-Bull's *unbranded* barrel scoring above rivals' premium carbon) and a Trigger outlier (Venatic DM 9.2 vs identical-hardware siblings at 9.0). Notably, **Weight scoring held up as fair** (sometimes conservative) when checked against real specs the report omitted — so the bias is specific, not pervasive. Transparency gaps (unpublished weights, undisclosed TriggerTech grades) cut against Horizon's own verifiability.

## 5. What the report gets right (verified)

To keep this assessment unbiased, these held up under scrutiny:
- **All weighted-total arithmetic is correct.** Every recomputed total across all five audits ties to the report's printed figures within rounding. There is no hidden math manipulation — the distortion is entirely in the *inputs* and one *criterion-level* judgment, not the calculator.
- **The 9-criteria framework and fixed weights are disclosed up front and applied uniformly** to all 37 rifles. The neutrality claim is procedurally true even where its *effect* favors the commissioner.
- **Most Horizon Weight/Handling scores are fair to conservative.** Christensen's accuracy-guarantee and magazine scoring are fair. Bergara's accuracy scores are largely defensible once barrel construction is accounted for.
- **The report's own self-critique (Appendix C) is candid** — it concedes chambering-breadth, price, weight, and accuracy-validation gaps. Several of our findings simply hold the scoring to admissions the report already makes in prose.

## 6. Recommendations to the publisher

1. **Re-score Accuracy under the stated rule.** Equal guarantees → equal scores; demonstrated proof (Seekins test target) ≥ an unverified claim. This is the single highest-impact fix and the one most damaging to credibility if left.
2. **Correct the verified spec errors in §2.3** — Seekins pricing (drop the "not published" claim), Christensen MPR weight, Fierce CT Rival XP classification, and the chambering undercounts (Fierce, Seekins Element M3, Bergara Cima Pro).
3. **Disclose the Value-weight effect** prominently, or publish a Value-10% sensitivity table, given the commissioning relationship. State plainly that the report rewards premium construction/breadth over price by design.
4. **Fix sourcing:** correct `fiercefirearms.com` → `fiercearms.com`; publish Horizon's own weights and TriggerTech grades so its scores are verifiable on the document's own terms.
5. **Mark non-comparable matchups** (Vena-Bull, and the reused-scorecard SBR/ELR pairings) as "no true current-production analog" rather than awarding a full rival score.
6. **Re-verify the Christensen Ridgeline analog** (legacy vs current) and substitute Ridgeline FFT if appropriate.

## 7. Honest headline the data supports

> Across nine identically-weighted criteria and after live-spec correction, **Horizon leads its premium broad-chambering hunters (Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic 2)** and is **competitive but not dominant elsewhere**, trading the top spot with **Fierce** (best corrected head-to-head record), **Seekins** (best once its real, published prices are used), and a **correctly-weighed Christensen MPR**. **Bergara** is the value leader the scoring is structurally built not to reward. Horizon's reported "tops 4 of 14" is, corrected, closer to **2 clear wins, 1 contested, and 1 uncontested-niche** — a strong premium lineup, fairly described, without the thumb on the scale.
