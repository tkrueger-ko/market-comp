# Fierce Firearms — Customer Sentiment / Delivery Audit

*Promise-vs-delivery study. Question: to what degree does the market believe Fierce actually DELIVERS on its 0.5–0.75 MOA accuracy and "premium quality" promise? Scope: Carbon Rogue (0.75 MOA), CT Rival XP (0.5), Carbon Rage LR (0.5), MTN Reaper 2.0 (0.5), Reaper H-TAC 2.0 (0.5). Research date: 2026-06-30.*

## Summary

- **Fierce is genuinely divisive, not uniformly good or bad.** The dominant grassroots verdict across multiple independent forums (Rokslide, Long Range Hunting, Long Range Only, Sniper's Hide) is a "roll the dice — you get a shooter or a boat anchor" reputation: real sub-MOA successes coexist with real, well-documented out-of-box failures. The brand "generally has a bad rap around this bunch" on Rokslide, and is repeatedly ranked **below Seekins** (cited as cheaper, more consistent, better service) and roughly **even with Christensen Arms** on QC variance.
- **The accuracy guarantee is achievable and frequently met — but mainly with factory match ammo, on a 3-shot basis, and the all-load average often misses spec.** Several genuine owners and press tests beat 0.5–0.75 MOA; a recurring minority (concentrated on in-scope **Carbon Rogue 7PRC** and **Mountain Reaper 7PRC** in 2023–24) report rifles that wouldn't group at all, fixed only by a warranty barrel swap. A third-party gunsmith reportedly saw "no less than 5 Fierce rifles" in one year with the same barrel problem, several re-barreled with Proof.
- **Customer service is the most consistent complaint when something goes wrong** — multi-week-to-multi-month silence on warranty channels, and a structural friction point: the accuracy-guarantee process charges the customer **$200 + shipping** if Fierce can replicate the guaranteed group in its own testing. A real but smaller minority report fast, even superior service (parts shipped next-day, "top notch" rebarrel turnaround).
- **The four newest in-scope SKUs (CT Rival XP, Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0) are effectively unproven in public owner reporting.** Almost all organic owner data covers older Rogue/Reaper/Rival/Edge/Fury rifles. The only in-scope CT Rival XP owner post said "shoots great" with no number; Carbon Rage LR yielded **zero** owner group data. Treat confident accuracy claims about these specific models as unverified.
- **A real manufacturing inflection exists:** Fierce's 2014–2021 partnership with a Canadian maker (later Antler Arms) ended in 2021, the same year Fierce ran a NIST-MEP Six Sigma/Kanban quality-improvement program. A consistent (owner-speculated, unconfirmed) "Canada-made good / Utah-made worse" decline narrative recurs across 3+ threads. Complaint threads with near-identical issues nonetheless continued into 2024 ("Yet another Fierce Firearms Issue...2024"), so any turnaround is partial/contested, not a clean before/after.

## Evidence volume & confidence

- **Approximate distinct sources / data points: ~45–50.** Roughly 30+ genuine independent owner posts across ~12 forum threads (Rokslide dominant, plus Long Range Hunting, Long Range Only, Hunt Talk, Sniper's Hide, 24hourcampfire); ~8–9 gun-press/independent reviews (Outdoor Life, Outdoor Canada, Athlon, Backfire.tv, all4shooters, GunMart, Cold Bore Miracle); plus institutional sources (BBB profile, NIST-MEP case study, Antler Arms history, a manufacturer recall notice).
- **Overall confidence: MEDIUM.** The core conclusion — *polarized/inconsistent QC + frequently slow CS, with an accuracy guarantee that is real but variable* — is corroborated across many independent threads and multiple years (2015–2024), which is robust. But three caveats lower confidence: (1) **Reddit yielded essentially nothing** (the crawler was blocked and Fierce is barely discussed there) — a real coverage gap; (2) forum data **skews to complaint threads** (selection bias toward negatives); (3) most **positive** evidence is gun-press reviews with undisclosed loaner/manufacturer-access status, so they cannot be weighted as independent verification.
- **Per-dimension confidence:** Accuracy = Medium (good for Rogue/Reaper, Low for the four newest SKUs). Build quality = Medium-High (strongly corroborated pattern). Warranty/CS = Medium (strong recurring pattern; no usable BBB complaint log or real Trustpilot profile found). Value = Low-Medium. Reputation = Medium. **Barrel life = Low** (almost no independent round-count data).

## Composite Delivery Score (weighted arithmetic shown)

```
Accuracy-in-practice      6.0 × 0.30 = 1.80
Build quality & reliab.   5.0 × 0.30 = 1.50
Warranty & cust. service  4.0 × 0.20 = 0.80
Value-for-money           5.0 × 0.10 = 0.50
Reputation / recommend    4.5 × 0.10 = 0.45
---------------------------------------------
Composite Delivery Score             = 5.05 / 10
```

**Composite ≈ 5.0 / 10 — "delivers inconsistently."** The market believes Fierce *can* deliver on the promise and frequently does for owners who get a good copy and feed match ammo, but enough owners receive rifles that miss the guarantee — compounded by slow/friction-laden customer service — that the brand's delivery is widely regarded as a gamble rather than a guarantee. **Confidence: MEDIUM.**

## Top praised themes (with sources)

1. **Accuracy potential is real and sometimes outstanding.** Sub-0.5 to 0.22 MOA reports from genuine owners and press: Carbon Rogue 0.21" best (outdoorcanada.ca); Carbon Rival 0.22 MOA "cloverleaf at 200" (rokslide.com/...fierce-7prc-carbon-rival); Mini-Rogue ".22 MOA" (longrangeonly.com/...has-the-quality-gone-up).
2. **Light weight, carbon barrels, and feature set are loved when the rifle is right.** "Insanely accurate... instant favorite" MTN Reaper (rokslide.com/...fierce-reaper-or-mtn-reaper); "one of the finest rifles I've ever shot" (backfire.tv); Outdoor Life CT Rival "ejected every case positively" over ~400 rounds.
3. **A genuine minority experience excellent customer service.** Next-day parts, 5-minute email responses, "top notch" rebarrel turnaround (longrangehunting.com 292683; rokslide.com/...fierce-7prc-carbon-rival) — fast resolution does happen, it's just inconsistent.
4. **Satisfied repeat/long-term owners exist**, especially older Canadian-made Fury/Rival owners ("zero issues") and some 2024 Carbon Rival buyers ("shoots lights out") (rokslide.com/...how-is-fierce-doing).

## Top complaint / concern themes (with sources)

1. **Unit-to-unit QC inconsistency ("get a lemon" / "boat anchor")** — the single strongest signal, recurring across ≥4 independent threads (snipershide.com 7214562; rokslide.com/...carbon-rival-quality; rokslide.com/...fierce-carbon-rogue).
2. **Carbon-barrel / tight-chamber accuracy failures out of box**, concentrated on in-scope Carbon Rogue 7PRC and Mountain Reaper 7PRC, sometimes only fixable by warranty re-barrel; a gunsmith reportedly saw ~5 such rifles in a year (longrangeonly.com/...fierce-firearm-accuracy-issue; rokslide.com/...yet-another-fierce-firearms-issue-2024).
3. **Slow / unresponsive warranty customer service** — multi-week to multi-month silence is the most repeated CS complaint (rokslide.com/...another-fierce-firearms-issue; longrangehunting.com/...customer-service; rokslide.com/...fierce-firearms-disaster).
4. **The $200 contingent accuracy-guarantee fee** — owners feel it discourages legitimate claims and means the guarantee isn't truly "free" to invoke (fiercearms.com/accuracy-warranty-form; rokslide.com/...how-is-fierce-doing).
5. **Fit/finish workmanship on carbon/stock components** — resin-clogged QD cups, canted muzzle brakes, Cerakote color mis-spec, "looked like crap for an almost $3k rifle" (rokslide.com/...another-fierce-firearms-issue; longrangeonly.com/...fierce-ct-edge-issues).
6. **Feeding/extraction: broken extractors/ejectors and stuck cases from tight chambers** (rokslide.com/...fierce-reaper-6-5-prc-pressure-problems; rokslide.com/...fierce-carbon-rogue).
7. **Reputational deficit vs Seekins** — repeatedly recommended *instead of* Fierce (rokslide.com/...fierce-vs-seekins-vs-bergara; snipershide.com 6997585).

## Delivers-on-guarantee verdict

**Mixed — the guarantee is real and often met, but not reliably enough to call it dependable, and the newest in-scope models are unproven.**

- **Evidence owners MEET the guarantee:** Multiple genuine owners and press tests beat the spec with factory match ammo — Carbon Rogue beating its 0.75 MOA (outdoorcanada.ca, backfire.tv 0.751"), Carbon Rival at 0.22–0.6 MOA (rokslide.com/...fierce-7prc-carbon-rival; longrangeonly.com), older Fury/Edge owners calling the "1/2 MOA guarantee legit" (hunttalk.com 1-2-moa thread). These are credible.
- **Evidence owners MISS the guarantee:** Equally credible independent reports of rifles delivering 2.5 MOA, "6 inch spreads at 50 yards," or "flyers that won't hit paper," requiring warranty re-barrels to fix — concentrated on in-scope Carbon Rogue 7PRC and Mountain Reaper 7PRC in 2023–24 (rokslide.com/...yet-another-fierce-firearms-issue-2024; longrangeonly.com/...fierce-firearm-accuracy-issue; rokslide.com/...how-is-fierce-doing). Notably, one owner reported Fierce's **own factory test target measured 0.64 MOA** on a 0.5-MOA-class rifle (rokslide.com/...another-fierce-firearms-issue).
- **Structural caveats on the guarantee itself:** it is a **3-shot group with recommended match ammo**, original-owner / ~1-year limited, and Fierce charges **$200 + shipping** if it can replicate the guaranteed group — friction that itself generates complaints and likely suppresses some reported failures.
- **Coverage gap:** CT Rival XP, Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, and Reaper H-TAC 2.0 have almost no organic owner group data; the H-TAC's only numbers come from a manufacturer-hosted press event. The guarantee's real-world delivery on these four specific SKUs is **unverified**.

**Net:** Fierce delivers on its accuracy promise often enough to keep a satisfied base, but with wide enough variance — and slow enough recovery when it fails — that the market treats the promise as conditional rather than guaranteed.
