# Fierce Firearms — Audit

## Summary

- The single largest, cleanest problem found: every Fierce rifle carrying a **0.5 MOA guarantee** (MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0, Carbon Rage LR) is capped at an **Accuracy score of 8.6 in all six matchups it appears in**, while Horizon's own 0.5-MOA models score **9.0–9.2** in those same matchups — for an *identical* published guarantee. This is the report's own stated rule ("published guarantee and precision construction") applied inconsistently, and it recurs too uniformly to be incidental.
- **Fierce CT Rival XP is misclassified.** The report's appendix lists it as "Steel · CT twisted/carbon," ~7 lb, 0.75 MOA. The live fiercearms.com product page (fetched directly) shows it is actually a **titanium-action, full C3 carbon-barrel** rifle with a **0.5 MOA guarantee** and a **6 lb** starting weight — Fierce sells a separate, genuinely steel "Twisted Rival XP" model that the report did not use. This mischaracterization understates CT Rival XP's Accuracy, Barrel, and Weight/Handling scores in all four matchups where it is the named rival (Venatic 2, Vandal Prime 2, Vandal X, Vandal Dark II).
- Chambering lists for **all five** Fierce models in the report's appendix list roughly half the calibers Fierce currently offers (verified directly against fiercearms.com), which likely understates the Caliber/chambering criterion modestly across the board.
- Not every issue cuts in Fierce's favor: the report's MTN Reaper 2.0 MSRP (≈$2,795) is **understated** versus the current official starting price (~$2,999–$3,199), which inflates its reported Value score. The report's Carbon Rogue magazine ("hinged floorplate") and most other specs check out as accurate.
- Net effect of correcting only the verified errors: two matchups (Venatic DM, Vandal Carbon 2) flip from "Seekins Element M3 tops" to "Fierce tops," and the Vandal Prime 2 matchup — currently shown as a clear 0.20-point Horizon win — narrows to a virtual statistical tie (0.04). No matchup flips against Fierce.
- The report's own Sources appendix cites "fiercefirearms.com" for Fierce data; that domain does not resolve (DNS failure). The real site is fiercearms.com — a minor but real citation defect that undercuts the report's claim of being built on verifiable "published manufacturer specifications."

## Findings

### 1. Identical 0.5 MOA guarantees scored systematically lower for Fierce than for Horizon
**Severity:** High
**Type:** scoring-inconsistency
**Location:** Accuracy criterion (20% weight) in the Venatic DM (p.06), Vandal DM (p.09), Venatic Max (p.11), Vandal Carbon 2 (p.08), and Vena-Bull (p.17) matchups.
**Report claims:** Fierce MTN Reaper 2.0 / Reaper H-TAC 2.0 / Carbon Rage LR — all published with a 0.5 MOA guarantee per the report's own appendix — score exactly **8.6** on Accuracy in every one of these five matchups. Horizon's own 0.5 MOA models in the same matchups score **9.0** (Venatic DM, Vandal DM, Venatic Max, Vandal Carbon 2) or **9.2** (Vena-Bull).
**Correct/verified:** Confirmed live: MTN Reaper 2.0 = 0.5 MOA (fiercearms.com/firearm/reaper-models/mtn-reaper-2-0/), Reaper H-TAC 2.0 = 0.5 MOA (fiercearms.com/firearm/reaper-models/reaper-h-tac-2-0/), Carbon Rage LR = 0.5 MOA (fiercearms.com/firearm/rage-models/carbon-rage-lr/). The guarantees are genuinely identical to Horizon's claim, not merely similar. (Note: the Venatic Riff matchup is the fair control case — Horizon's own 0.5 MOA SBR also scores only 8.6 there, matching Fierce. That one is internally consistent and is *not* flagged.)
**Fairness impact:** The report's stated rule is "published guarantee and precision construction; guarantees are manufacturer claims, not measured results" — i.e., a tighter guarantee should not by itself separate two rifles. A flat, unexplained 0.4–0.6 point gap between identical guarantees, recurring across five separate matchups, is the clearest evidence in the report of a thumb on the scale against Fierce.
**Proposed score correction:** Raise Fierce's Accuracy score from 8.6 → 9.0 in the five matchups listed (matching the Horizon baseline for an identical guarantee; Vena-Bull's 9.2 is not fully extended to Reaper H-TAC 2.0 since ELR-specific "precision construction" claims are harder to verify, so 9.0 is used there too as the conservative figure).

Recomputed weighted totals (Accuracy weight = 20%, so Δ0.4 × 0.20 = +0.08 to weighted total):
| Matchup | Model | Old Accuracy → New | Old total | New total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Venatic DM | Fierce MTN Reaper 2.0 | 8.6 → 9.0 | 8.62 | **8.70** |
| Vandal DM | Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0 | 8.6 → 9.0 | 8.59 | **8.67** |
| Venatic Max | Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0 | 8.6 → 9.0 | 8.59 | **8.67** |
| Vena-Bull | Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0 | 8.6 → 9.0 | 8.59 | **8.67** |
| Vandal Carbon 2 | Fierce Carbon Rage LR | 8.6 → 9.0 | 8.58 | **8.66** |

In Venatic DM, the corrected 8.70 now exceeds both Seekins Element M3 (8.66, the report's stated matchup winner) and Horizon's own Venatic DM (8.64) — Fierce becomes the top-scoring rifle. In Vandal Carbon 2, the corrected 8.66 ties Seekins Element M3 (8.66) exactly, displacing it as sole top scorer.

### 2. CT Rival XP is mischaracterized as a steel rifle; it is actually Fierce's titanium/full-carbon model
**Severity:** High
**Type:** spec-error / analog-rule-distortion
**Location:** Appendix A Fierce table (p.19); used as the named rival in Venatic 2 (p.05), Vandal Prime 2 (p.07), Vandal X (p.13), Vandal Dark II (p.14).
**Report claims:** "CT Rival XP ... Steel · CT twisted/carbon ... 0.75 MOA ... ~7 lb."
**Correct/verified:** fiercearms.com/firearm/rival-models/ct-rival-xp/ states the action is a "Dual-Lug **Titanium** Action," the barrel is a "Fierce **C3 Match Grade carbon** barrel," the guarantee is "**.5 MOA**," and weight is "Starting at **6 lbs**." Fierce sells a separate, genuinely steel-barreled "Twisted Rival XP" (fiercearms.com/firearm/rival-models/twisted-rival-xp/, "Fierce Spiral-Fluted Stainless Steel Match Grade Barrel," "Dual-Lug Stainless-Steel Action") that the report did not use.
**Fairness impact:** The report's own method says "Fierce's carbon-only core line is used as its nearest analog" when matching against Horizon steel rifles — so using CT Rival XP for the four steel Vandal/Venatic models is actually *consistent* with the stated rule once you know it's a carbon/Ti gun. The unfairness is that the spec sheet feeding the scores describes it as if it were the steel model, which caps three criteria (Accuracy, Barrel, Weight/Handling) at steel-tier values instead of the carbon-tier values every other genuine Fierce carbon rifle receives elsewhere in the same report (Barrel 8.7–8.8, Weight/Handling 8.5–9.0 for Carbon Rogue/Rage LR/Reaper models, vs. 8.5/8.0–8.4 given to CT Rival XP).
**Proposed score correction:** Accuracy 8.4 → 8.6 (consistent with Finding 1's corrected Fierce 0.5-MOA baseline); Barrel 8.5 → 8.7 (matching Carbon Rogue's carbon-barrel score, the closest comparable Fierce carbon barrel); Weight/Handling 8.4 → 8.8 (interpolating between Carbon Rogue's 5.8 lb/9.0 and Reaper H-TAC's 6.5–6.7 lb/8.5 for CT Rival XP's verified 6 lb).

Recomputed weighted total (CT Rival XP scores identically across all four of its matchups, so one calculation applies to all):
- Accuracy: 0.20 × (8.6 − 8.4) = +0.040
- Barrel: 0.09 × (8.7 − 8.5) = +0.018
- Weight/Handling: 0.15 × (8.8 − 8.4) = +0.060
- Total correction: **+0.118**, old total 8.41 → **new total ≈ 8.53** (before the chambering correction in Finding 4, which adds a further +0.04 → **≈8.57**)

| Matchup | Horizon score | Fierce CT Rival XP: old → corrected | Result before correction | Result after correction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Venatic 2 | 8.71 | 8.41 → 8.57 | Horizon wins by 0.30 | Horizon wins by 0.14 |
| Vandal Prime 2 | 8.61 | 8.41 → 8.57 | Horizon wins by 0.20 | **Horizon wins by 0.04 — effectively a tie** |
| Vandal X | 8.12 | 8.41 → 8.57 | Fierce already wins by 0.29 | Fierce wins by 0.45 |
| Vandal Dark II | 8.06 | 8.41 → 8.57 | Fierce already wins by 0.35 | Fierce wins by 0.51 |

### 3. Carbon Rogue's tighter guarantee scored below a looser Bergara guarantee in one matchup
**Severity:** Medium
**Type:** scoring-inconsistency
**Location:** Accuracy criterion, Venatic Carbon 2 matchup (p.04).
**Report claims:** Fierce Carbon Rogue (0.75 MOA) scores 8.3 on Accuracy — *below* Bergara Premier Cima Pro (1.0 MOA guarantee per Appendix A2), which scores 8.4.
**Correct/verified:** No new external fact needed — this is an internal inconsistency. In the Vandal 2 and Villain matchups (same two rifles, same criterion), Carbon Rogue (8.3) correctly outscores Bergara B-14 Wilderness/Sq. Crest (8.0–8.1), consistent with its tighter, more specific guarantee. Only in Venatic Carbon 2 is the order reversed.
**Fairness impact:** A rifle with a numerically tighter accuracy guarantee should not score lower than a rival with a looser one under the report's own stated rubric, absent an explained "precision construction" offset (none is given). This appears to be a copy/scoring slip rather than a pattern, but it directly disadvantages Fierce in its single highest-profile matchup (the report's #1 model, Venatic Carbon 2).
**Proposed score correction:** Accuracy 8.3 → 8.4 (restoring the ordering consistent with Carbon Rogue's own treatment elsewhere). Weighted-total impact: 0.20 × 0.1 = +0.02, old total 8.44 → **new total 8.46**. This does not change the matchup's named winner (Venatic Carbon 2, 8.84) but it does flip Carbon Rogue from narrowly *behind* Bergara Premier Cima Pro (8.44 vs 8.45) to narrowly *ahead* of it (8.46 vs 8.45) among the rivals shown.

### 4. Chambering lists understate Fierce's actual current breadth by roughly half across all five models
**Severity:** Medium
**Type:** spec-error
**Location:** Appendix A (p.19) and all matchups using a Fierce rival for the Caliber/chambering criterion (10% weight).
**Report claims:** Carbon Rogue 9 calibers; CT Rival XP 4; Carbon Rage LR 7; MTN Reaper 2.0 5; Reaper H-TAC 2.0 4–5.
**Correct/verified:** Live fiercearms.com model pages show 11, 7, 10, 10, and 10 calibers respectively (see spec table above; sources as cited there). In every case the live list is longer than what the report counted, and in two cases (MTN Reaper 2.0's "6mm CM," CT Rival XP's "6.5 CM" and "28 Nosler") the report includes calibers that are not on the current live list — suggesting the appendix reflects an older or hand-compiled snapshot rather than current pages, despite the report's own claim that figures should be "confirmed at publication."
**Fairness impact:** Caliber/chambering is explicitly scored on "breadth of chamberings orderable today." An undercount this large (often ~2x) plausibly suppresses Fierce's score on a criterion the report itself flags as decisive for several Horizon models. However, the report does not publish a numeric formula linking caliber count to score (comparable listed-counts already produce inconsistent scores across brands elsewhere in the report, e.g., Seekins' 6 listed calibers score 8.6 while Christensen's 6 listed calibers score 7.8), so an exact corrected figure cannot be derived mechanically.
**Proposed score correction (directional, conservative):** Caliber score +0.2 to +0.4 for each Fierce model, reflecting that verified real breadth (7–11 options) is now closer to or exceeds several rivals' listed counts rather than trailing them:
- Carbon Rogue: 8.4 → 8.6 (weighted-total impact 0.10 × 0.2 = +0.02; applies in Venatic Carbon 2, Vandal 2, Villain matchups)
- CT Rival XP: 8.0 → 8.4 (0.10 × 0.4 = +0.04; already folded into Finding 2's combined total of ≈8.57)
- Carbon Rage LR: 8.4 → 8.6 (0.10 × 0.2 = +0.02; Vandal Carbon 2 matchup, stacks with Finding 1: 8.66 → **8.68**, now exceeding Seekins Element M3's 8.66)
- MTN Reaper 2.0: 8.0 → 8.4 (0.10 × 0.4 = +0.04; Venatic DM matchup stacks with Finding 1: 8.70 → **8.74**; Venatic Riff matchup: 8.62 → **8.66**)
- Reaper H-TAC 2.0: 8.0 → 8.4 (0.10 × 0.4 = +0.04; Vandal DM/Venatic Max/Vena-Bull matchups each stack with Finding 1: 8.67 → **8.71**)

### 5. MTN Reaper 2.0's MSRP is understated, which inflates its reported Value score — a correction *against* Fierce
**Severity:** Low
**Type:** spec-error
**Location:** Appendix A (p.19); Venatic DM (p.06) and Venatic Riff (p.10) matchups, Value criterion (5% weight).
**Report claims:** MTN Reaper 2.0 ≈$2,795.
**Correct/verified:** fiercearms.com/firearm/reaper-models/mtn-reaper-2-0/ states "Starting at $3,199"; the configurator/shop page (fiercearms.com/mtn-reaper/) shows a $2,999–$4,999 range depending on build. The report's figure is roughly $200–400 below the current floor price.
**Fairness impact:** This is the one verified error that does **not** favor Fierce — a lower assumed price makes the Value criterion ("feature set relative to MSRP") look better for Fierce than current pricing supports. Flagging it is part of holding this audit to a "truth and fairness" standard rather than a pro-Fierce one.
**Proposed score correction:** Value 7.8 → 7.5 in both matchups it appears in (Venatic DM, Venatic Riff). Weighted-total impact: 0.05 × (−0.3) = −0.015. Combined with Finding 1 and Finding 4's MTN Reaper 2.0 corrections: Venatic DM total 8.74 → **8.73**; Venatic Riff total 8.66 → **8.65**. Neither changes which rifle tops its matchup (Fierce already topped both, before and after all corrections).

### 6. Carbon Rogue's trigger spec is outdated
**Severity:** Low
**Type:** spec-error
**Location:** Appendix A (p.19), referenced narratively at multiple points in the user's brief and the report's Sources section.
**Report claims:** "Note the Rogue uses a Bix'n Andy Dakota trigger."
**Correct/verified:** The current fiercearms.com Carbon Rogue product page lists "Trigger Tech Primary Pro Curved" as the standard trigger, with no mention of Bix'n Andy. Independent review/dealer sources confirm Bix'n Andy Dakota was used on earlier production and that Fierce has been transitioning rifles to TriggerTech; some legacy/dealer-stock serial numbers still show Bix'n Andy (e.g., store.prophetriver.com listing).
**Fairness impact:** Ambiguous/transitional — the report's claim was likely accurate at an earlier point but is not for current (2026) production per the live spec page. Carbon Rogue's Trigger score (8.6) is already the highest among its listed rivals in every matchup it appears in, so it is not clear this error has actually suppressed its score; no clean basis exists for a specific point correction either way.
**Proposed score correction:** None proposed (insufficient basis to determine direction or magnitude). Recommend the report simply update the spec line to reflect current production.

### 7. Cited Fierce source domain does not resolve
**Severity:** Low
**Type:** spec-error (sourcing)
**Location:** Appendix B, Sources (p.22): "Fierce Firearms — fiercefirearms.com (Carbon Rogue, CT Rival XP, Carbon Rage LR, MTN Reaper 2.0, Reaper H-TAC 2.0)."
**Report claims:** fiercefirearms.com is the source for all Fierce specifications used in scoring.
**Correct/verified:** Direct fetch of fiercefirearms.com returns a DNS resolution failure (the domain does not exist/resolve). Fierce's actual official site is fiercearms.com.
**Fairness impact:** Does not change any score, but undermines the report's claim of transparent, checkable sourcing — a reader attempting to verify Fierce's specs via the cited URL cannot do so.
**Proposed score correction:** None (citation fix only).

### 8. Role-matching note: Vena-Bull vs. Reaper H-TAC 2.0 is a poor analog, but it does not disadvantage Fierce
**Severity:** Informational
**Type:** analog-rule-distortion
**Location:** Vena-Bull matchup (p.17).
**Report claims:** Vena-Bull ($9,999, ELR platform, .375/.408 CheyTac, 30" barrel) is matched against Fierce Reaper H-TAC 2.0 (~$2,699, hunting/PRS chassis, 6mm Creedmoor–.300 PRC class).
**Fairness impact:** This is a genuine role/price/caliber-class mismatch (3.7x price gap, no chambering overlap), and it is the only Fierce-named matchup with essentially no "current production, role-matched" rivals available, which the report's own Vena-Bull copy half-acknowledges ("purpose-built extreme long-range platform with limited direct rivals in this group"). It does not disadvantage Fierce — Fierce wins this matchup even before any of the corrections above (and wins by more after Finding 1's correction) — but it means this particular "Fierce tops" result is not load-bearing evidence of head-to-head superiority; it mainly reflects that Vena-Bull has no real peer in the comparison set.
**Proposed score correction:** None — flagged for transparency, not because it requires a number change.

## Net effect on rankings

Applying only the verified, quantified corrections above (Findings 1–5; Findings 6–8 are non-scoring):

- **Matchups that flip to Fierce:** Venatic DM (Seekins Element M3 8.66 → Fierce MTN Reaper 2.0 **8.74** tops) and Vandal Carbon 2 (Seekins Element M3 8.66 → Fierce Carbon Rage LR **8.68** tops). Seekins Element M3 loses both of its "tops the matchup" results to Fierce once Fierce's own specs are corrected.
- **Matchups that narrow sharply but don't flip:** Vandal Prime 2 goes from a 0.20-point Horizon win to a 0.04-point (statistically immaterial) Horizon win.
- **Matchups where Fierce's existing win grows:** Venatic Riff, Vandal DM, Venatic Max, Vena-Bull, Vandal X, Vandal Dark II, Vandal 2, Villain — all already-named Fierce wins, with corrected margins 0.04–0.16 points larger.
- **No matchup flips against Fierce.** The one correction that cuts against Fierce (MTN Reaper 2.0's understated MSRP, Finding 5) is too small (−0.015) to offset the gains from Findings 1 and 4 in the same matchups.
- **Headline-index effect:** the report's "Horizon tops 4 of 14" / "Fierce tops in matchups 6,7,8,9,10,11,12,14 (8 of 14)" framing would become, after correction, **Fierce tops 10 of 14**, Horizon tops 2 clearly (Venatic Carbon 2, Venatic 2) plus a near-tie in Vandal Prime 2 and its own win in Vandal Bloodline (a non-Fierce matchup), and **Seekins tops 0 of 14** (down from 2).
